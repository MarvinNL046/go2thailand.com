## A Different Kind of Songkran

Most tourists experience Songkran between April 13 and 15, but if you miss those dates — or simply want something more traditional — Phra Pradaeng in Samut Prakan Province holds its own celebration a week later. In 2026 the festival runs from April 24 to 26, following a local Mon-heritage calendar that has been observed for generations.

Phra Pradaeng sits just across the Chao Phraya River from central Bangkok, making it one of the easiest cultural day trips in the entire country.

## What Makes It Special

Unlike the high-energy water battles on Khao San Road or Silom, Phra Pradaeng Songkran is rooted in the traditions of Thailand's Mon community. The Mon people settled in this area centuries ago, and their descendants still preserve customs that set this festival apart from mainstream celebrations.

Key highlights include:

- **Nang Songkran procession** — a colourful parade featuring participants in traditional Thai-Raman attire, floats and community groups marching through the district.
- **Saba folk games** — an ancient Mon pastime where teams toss a wooden disc, turning sport into a social bonding ritual.
- **Mon "Thae Yam" songs** — local folk music performed live during the festivities, creating an atmosphere you will not find anywhere else in the Bangkok area.
- **Kalamae tasting** — a sticky, caramel-like confection unique to Phra Pradaeng that is freshly prepared and offered to visitors during the merit-making ceremonies.

Traditional rice soaked in jasmine-scented iced water, called "khao chae," is also widely served — a dish historically prepared to honour ancestors during the Thai New Year.

## How to Get There

The most popular way to reach Phra Pradaeng is by ferry across the Chao Phraya River, which avoids the traffic jams that build up on the roads. Several bus routes also pass near the event area:

- **Line 82** — Phra Pradaeng to Sanam Luang
- **Line 138** — Phra Pradaeng to Mo Chit 2
- **Line 506** — Pak Kret to Phra Pradaeng
- **Line 6** — Phra Pradaeng to Bang Lamphu

A taxi or Grab ride from central Bangkok takes roughly 30 to 45 minutes depending on traffic.

## Practical Tips

- **Dress modestly** during morning merit-making at local temples. The water play kicks off later in the day.
- **Bring a waterproof phone pouch.** Even though the splashing is gentler than Bangkok's main Songkran zones, you will still get wet.
- **Arrive early.** The parade usually begins mid-morning and the best viewing spots fill up fast.
- **Try the kalamae.** Vendors sell it freshly made in banana-leaf wraps near the Phra Pradaeng District Office.

If you want a Songkran experience that feels authentic rather than tourist-oriented, Phra Pradaeng is hard to beat — and it is practically on Bangkok's doorstep.
