While **Koh Chang** remains far quieter than its famous southern cousins, this 217-square-kilometer island in Thailand's eastern Trat Province is quietly becoming the choice of travelers seeking genuine beaches, jungle trails, and a pace of life that hasn't been entirely swallowed by tourism. Unlike the overcrowded party scenes of Koh Samui or the Instagram-saturated waters of Koh Phi Phi, Koh Chang offers something increasingly rare in Thailand: space to breathe.

## 1. Why Koh Chang Remains Thailand's Best-Kept Island Secret

**Koh Chang** translates to "Elephant Island," though you're unlikely to see wild elephants roaming the beaches. What you will find is a landscape that feels genuinely undeveloped compared to the overdeveloped resort islands of the south. The island's interior is blanketed in dense jungle, home to waterfalls, hiking trails, and a network of small villages where life moves at a pace dictated by tides and monsoons, not tour schedules.

When our team visited during the shoulder season, the difference was immediately apparent. While Koh Samui and Phuket were packed with tour groups and beachfront clubs, Koh Chang's main beaches—White Sand Beach and Lonely Beach—had space to walk without navigating a maze of sunbeds. This isn't accidental. The island has deliberately resisted the mega-resort development that transformed its southern neighbors, partly due to its designation as part of Mu Ko Chang National Marine Park.

The island is Thailand's second-largest after Phuket, yet it receives a fraction of the visitors. This paradox makes it ideal for travelers seeking authentic beach culture without the premium prices or the sensory overload. You'll find quality accommodation and food at prices that haven't been inflated by Instagram fame.

> **Did You Know?** Koh Chang became a national marine park in 1982, protecting 52 islands and over 6,000 hectares of marine habitat. This legal protection is one reason the island hasn't been carved up into mega-resorts.

## 2. Getting to Koh Chang: Your Transport Options

Reaching **Koh Chang** is straightforward, though it requires a bit more planning than flying directly to Phuket. The most common route is via Laem Ngop Pier in Trat Province, a coastal town 45 kilometers from Trat International Airport.

### Ferry Routes and Schedules

The ferry crossing takes 45 minutes to 1.5 hours depending on sea conditions and which pier you depart from. During our research, we found that ferries run multiple times daily, with frequency increasing during peak season (November–February). The standard fare is around 200 THB (~$5.50 USD) for a basic passenger ticket. Vehicle ferries cost significantly more if you're bringing a car or motorcycle.

**Pro Tip:** Book your ferry ticket the day before during high season, though walk-ups are usually accommodated. The ferry terminals can be chaotic during peak times, so arrive 30 minutes early.

### Getting from Bangkok or Other Cities

Most travelers reach Laem Ngop via a combination of bus and local transport. From Bangkok, you can take an overnight bus to Trat (around 6–7 hours, 400–600 THB), then a local songthaew (shared taxi) to Laem Ngop Pier (30 minutes, 50–100 THB). Alternatively, fly from Bangkok to Trat International Airport, then arrange a pickup or take a taxi to the pier.

## 3. Best Beaches: Where to Stay and What Each Offers

**Koh Chang** has distinct beach zones, each with its own character. Unlike Phuket, where beaches blur into one another, each beach here feels like a separate destination.

### White Sand Beach (Haad Sai Khao)

White Sand Beach is the island's most developed area, with the widest selection of restaurants, bars, and accommodation. The sand is genuinely white, and the beach stretches for several kilometers. During our visit in May, the beach was spacious enough to find quiet spots even in the afternoon. The water is calm and suitable for swimming year-round, though the monsoon season (May–October) brings occasional rough waves.

**Best For:** First-time visitors, families, those wanting walkable nightlife and dining options.

**Accommodation Range:** Budget bungalows (400–800 THB/night), mid-range resorts (1,500–3,000 THB/night), upscale beachfront properties (4,000+ THB/night).

### Lonely Beach (Haad Tha Nam)

Lonely Beach is the island's backpacker and party hub, though "party" here means live music and beach bars, not the hedonistic Full Moon Party atmosphere of Koh Phangan. It's younger, louder, and more social than White Sand Beach. Accommodation is budget-focused, and the vibe attracts solo travelers and groups.

**Best For:** Solo travelers, backpackers, those seeking social atmosphere without excessive partying.

**Accommodation Range:** Hostels and budget bungalows (300–600 THB/night), basic mid-range (1,000–2,000 THB/night).

### Klong Prao Beach

South of White Sand Beach, Klong Prao is quieter and more family-oriented. A freshwater stream runs behind the beach, creating a scenic backdrop. The beach itself is less crowded, and the vibe is distinctly more relaxed.

**Best For:** Families, couples seeking tranquility, those wanting fewer tourists.

**Accommodation Range:** Mid-range bungalows and resorts (1,500–3,500 THB/night).

### Bang Bao: The Authentic Fishing Village

Bang Bao is less a beach and more a working fishing village built on stilts over the water. It's where local life continues undisturbed by tourism. The pier is lined with seafood restaurants, dive shops, and small guesthouses. There's no sandy beach here—just weathered wooden platforms and the smell of the sea. This is the most authentic corner of Koh Chang.

**Best For:** Divers (several dive shops operate here), photographers, those seeking genuine Thai village life.

**Accommodation Range:** Basic guesthouses (600–1,500 THB/night).

## 4. Water [Activities](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK?subid=blog): Diving, [Snorkeling](https://klook.tpo.lv/7Dt6WApj?subid=blog), and Kayaking

**Koh Chang** sits within the Mu Ko Chang National Marine Park, making it an exceptional base for underwater exploration. The surrounding waters are home to coral reefs, tropical fish, and occasional larger marine life.

### Diving and Snorkeling

The island has several PADI-certified dive shops, primarily clustered around Bang Bao and White Sand Beach. Popular dive sites include Hin Lad Reef and the wreck of the HTMS Khram, a sunken naval vessel that has become an artificial reef. Visibility ranges from 10–20 meters depending on season, with the clearest water typically from November to April.

**Snorkeling** is accessible from most beaches, though dedicated snorkeling trips to nearby islands (Koh Wai, Koh Mak) offer better coral and fish diversity. A typical half-day snorkeling tour costs 800–1,500 THB per person.

**Pro Tip:** Book diving and snorkeling trips through your accommodation or directly at dive shops. Prices are consistent across operators, so choose based on group size and schedule rather than price.

### Kayaking and Island Hopping

Sea kayaking through the mangrove forests and around nearby islands is a quieter alternative to motorized tours. Several outfitters rent single and double kayaks (400–600 THB/day). The paddling is gentle, suitable for beginners, and offers close-up views of birdlife and coastal vegetation.

## 5. Jungle Trekking and Waterfalls

Beyond the beaches, **Koh Chang's** interior is a network of hiking trails leading to waterfalls and viewpoints. The most popular trek is to **Klong Plu Waterfall**, a two-tiered cascade accessible via a 1.5-kilometer trail from Klong Prao Beach. The walk takes about 30 minutes and passes through primary jungle.

### Popular Waterfall Hikes

The Klong Plu trail is well-marked and suitable for families with children. The waterfall itself is swimmable, and the pool is refreshingly cool even during the hot season. A second waterfall, Klong Khlong, is less visited and requires a longer trek (3–4 kilometers round trip) through denser jungle.

**Best Time:** Visit waterfalls during or just after the monsoon season (June–October) when water flow is highest. During the dry season (November–April), some falls reduce to a trickle.

Our team hiked to Klong Plu in May and found the jungle lush but humid—temperatures reached 32°C with high humidity, making an early morning start essential. The reward was nearly solitary access to the waterfall, as most tourists visit during the cooler months.

### Jungle Guides

Local guides are available through accommodation or directly at trailheads. A guide costs 500–800 THB for a small group and adds significant value, as they identify wildlife, explain the ecosystem, and know shortcuts. Guides are optional for well-marked trails like Klong Plu but essential for off-trail exploration.

## 6. Food and Drink: Local Flavors Without the Tourist Markup

**Koh Chang's** food scene reflects its dual nature: tourist-friendly restaurants on the main beaches and authentic local eateries in villages and smaller areas. Prices are noticeably lower than Phuket or Koh Samui.

### Beach Restaurants and Cafes

White Sand Beach and Lonely Beach have numerous restaurants serving Thai, international, and fusion cuisine. A typical meal costs 150–300 THB for Thai dishes, 250–400 THB for Western food. Quality varies, but most beachfront restaurants maintain decent standards.

### Local Markets and Street Food

The real food culture exists in small markets and street stalls. A bowl of khao tom (rice soup) or pad thai from a street vendor costs 40–80 THB. The night market near White Sand Beach (open evenings) offers grilled seafood, fresh fruit, and desserts at local prices. Trying local spots is not just cheaper—it's where you'll encounter actual Thai food, not the sanitized tourist version.

### Seafood and Fresh Catches

Bang Bao's waterfront restaurants specialize in fresh seafood. You can select your fish or prawns from tanks and have them grilled or prepared to order. A meal typically costs 300–600 THB per person, reasonable for fresh seafood in a scenic setting.

During our May visit, we sampled grilled mackerel and fresh crab at a small Bang Bao restaurant. The owner, a local fisherman, prepared the meal while sharing stories about the village's fishing traditions. This kind of authentic interaction is common on Koh Chang and rare on more developed islands.

### Drinks and Nightlife

Bars on Lonely Beach and White Sand Beach serve Thai beer (Singha, Chang, Leo) for 60–100 THB per bottle. Cocktails range from 100–200 THB, significantly cheaper than Phuket. The drinking culture is relaxed—no pressure to stay late or spend heavily.

## 7. Accommodation: From Budget Bungalows to Mid-Range Comfort

**Koh Chang** lacks ultra-luxury resorts, which is part of its charm. Accommodation is dominated by family-run bungalows, small resorts, and a growing number of mid-range properties.

### Budget Bungalows (300–800 THB/night)

Basic bungalows with fan cooling, shared or private bathrooms, and minimal amenities are abundant, especially on Lonely Beach and in smaller areas. Many are charming—wooden structures with porches overlooking gardens or partial sea views. For solo travelers and backpackers, these offer unbeatable value.

### Mid-Range Resorts (1,500–3,500 THB/night)

Mid-range properties typically include air conditioning, hot water, decent Wi-Fi, and sometimes a small pool or beachfront access. These are family-run or small Thai-owned chains, not international brands. Quality is generally good, and staff are attentive.

### Upscale Properties (4,000+ THB/night)

A handful of higher-end resorts exist, primarily on White Sand Beach and Klong Prao. These offer private pools, spa services, and fine dining. Even at this level, prices are lower than comparable properties in Phuket.

**Booking Tip:** During May–September (monsoon season), prices drop 20–30% from peak-season rates. Accommodation is abundant, and you can often negotiate weekly or monthly discounts.

## 8. When to Visit: Seasons and What to Expect

**Koh Chang's** climate follows Thailand's monsoon pattern. The island experiences three distinct seasons, each with different implications for travelers.

### Dry Season (November–April)

This is peak tourism season. Temperatures range from 25–32°C, humidity is lower, and rainfall is minimal. Seas are calm, making it ideal for diving, snorkeling, and water activities. Beaches are busier, and prices are at their highest. If you visit during this period, book accommodation in advance, especially around Thai holidays.

### Monsoon Season (May–October)

May through September brings heavy rainfall, particularly in the afternoons. Temperatures remain warm (28–35°C), but humidity is oppressive. Many travelers avoid this season, but our experience suggests it's underrated. Rain typically falls in short bursts, leaving mornings and early afternoons clear. Beaches are nearly empty, prices drop significantly, and the jungle is at its most lush.

**Caveat:** Some smaller guesthouses close during monsoon season, and a few water activities may be curtailed on particularly rough days. Diving is still possible, though visibility decreases.

### Shoulder Seasons (April–May, September–October)

These transitional months offer a balance: fewer crowds than peak season, lower prices, and generally good weather. April can be hot and humid; October still sees occasional rain but is drying out.

> **Did You Know?** Thailand's monsoon season (May–October) is when the island receives 80% of its annual rainfall, yet it remains warm year-round. Temperatures rarely drop below 25°C even during the coolest months.

## 9. Day Trips and Nearby Attractions

**Koh Chang** is part of an archipelago. Several nearby islands are accessible via short boat trips and offer distinct experiences.

### Koh Wai and Koh Mak

These smaller islands, 30–45 minutes by boat, are even quieter than Koh Chang. Koh Wai is rocky and dramatic, popular with snorkelers. Koh Mak is flatter and more developed, with a few resorts and a village. Day trips typically cost 600–1,200 THB per person and include snorkeling and lunch.

### Koh Kood

Further south, Koh Kood is Thailand's fourth-largest island and feels even more remote. A full-day trip involves a 2-hour boat journey, making it a commitment, but the reward is pristine beaches and minimal tourism infrastructure. This is a destination for those seeking genuine solitude.

### Trat Town

The mainland town of Trat, 45 kilometers away, is worth a half-day visit. It's a working Thai town with markets, temples, and local restaurants untouched by tourism. The old town has colonial-era shophouses and a relaxed atmosphere. Most travelers skip Trat, but it offers authentic cultural immersion.

## 10. Practical Information: Money, Safety, and Logistics

### Money and Costs

**Koh Chang** is genuinely affordable. A daily budget of 800–1,200 THB (~$22–33 USD) covers basic accommodation, street food meals, and local transport. Mid-range travelers spending 2,000–3,000 THB/day (~$55–83 USD) enjoy comfortable accommodation, restaurant meals, and activities. ATMs are available on White Sand Beach and Lonely Beach, though fees may apply. Credit cards are accepted at mid-range and upscale establishments but not at street vendors or small shops.

### Safety

**Koh Chang** is safe by Thai standards. Petty theft is rare, violent crime is virtually nonexistent, and the island has a visible police presence. Standard precautions apply: avoid displaying expensive jewelry or cameras, be cautious with drinks at bars, and use registered taxis or ask your accommodation for transport recommendations.

### Internet and Connectivity

Wi-Fi is available at most accommodation and restaurants, though speeds can be slow during peak hours. [Mobile data](https://saily.tpo.lv/rf9lidnE?subid=blog) is reliable; both AIS and Dtac offer prepaid SIM cards (starting at 99 THB for basic plans). For longer stays, consider a monthly data package.

### Health and Medical Services

Basic medical facilities exist on the island, but serious medical emergencies require evacuation to Trat or Bangkok. Travel insurance is strongly recommended. Pharmacies are available on White Sand Beach for minor ailments.

### Getting Around the Island

Songthaews (shared taxis) connect beaches and villages, costing 30–50 THB per trip. Motorcycle rentals are available (200–300 THB/day) for independent exploration, though road conditions vary. Taxis can be arranged through accommodation for longer trips.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Koh Chang suitable for families with young children?

Yes. White Sand Beach and Klong Prao Beach have calm waters, shallow areas, and family-friendly accommodation. The island lacks the party atmosphere of Koh Phangan and has plenty of nature-based activities suitable for kids. Waterfall hikes and kayaking are family-friendly, though supervision is necessary.

### How does Koh Chang compare to Koh Samui or Koh Phangan?

Koh Chang is quieter, less developed, and significantly cheaper. It lacks the nightlife and resort luxury of Koh Samui but offers more authentic Thai culture and natural beauty. Unlike Koh Phangan, it's not a party destination. Choose Koh Chang for relaxation and nature; choose Koh Samui for resort amenities and nightlife.

### Can I visit Koh Chang during monsoon season?

Yes, though with caveats. Rain is frequent but usually brief. Prices are lower, crowds are minimal, and the landscape is lush. Some activities may be curtailed, and a few smaller guesthouses close. It's ideal for budget travelers and those seeking solitude.

### How long should I stay on Koh Chang?

Three to five days balances beach time, jungle exploration, and water activities. A week allows for deeper exploration of the island and nearby islands. Less than two days feels rushed.

### Is Koh Chang good for diving?

Yes. The island has several PADI-certified dive shops, and nearby reefs and wreck sites offer good diving. Visibility is best from November to April. Beginners can take open-water courses; experienced divers will find challenging sites.

### What's the best way to get to Koh Chang from Bangkok?

Take an overnight bus to Trat (6–7 hours, 400–600 THB), then a songthaew to Laem Ngop Pier (30 minutes, 50–100 THB), then a ferry to the island (45 minutes, ~200 THB). Alternatively, [fly to](https://trip.tpo.lv/TmObooZ5?subid=blog) Trat International Airport and arrange ground transport. Total journey time is 8–10 hours via bus or 3–4 hours via flight.

### Are there ATMs and banks on Koh Chang?

Yes, ATMs are available on White Sand Beach and Lonely Beach. Banks are limited; plan accordingly if you need to exchange large amounts of currency or conduct banking services.
