## Beyond Doi Suthep: The Temples Locals Actually Visit

Everyone who visits [Chiang Mai](/city/chiang-mai/) heads straight for [Wat Phra That Doi Suthep](/city/chiang-mai/attractions/doi-suthep/), and rightly so — the golden mountaintop temple is stunning. But the [Old City](/city/chiang-mai/attractions/old-city-chiang-mai/) and its surrounding hillside hide dozens of quieter Lanna temples where you can admire centuries-old architecture without jostling for photo space. Here are five hidden gems you can string together on a single walking day.

## 1. Wat Lok Moli — The Peaceful Starting Point

Begin your morning at Wat Lok Moli, tucked just inside the northern moat of the [Old City](/city/chiang-mai/attractions/old-city-chiang-mai/). Built in the 14th century under King Kuena, this temple is one of Chiang Mai's oldest and finest examples of classic Lanna architecture. Its towering brick chedi and dark-teak viharn are beautifully maintained, yet most mornings you will have the grounds almost to yourself. Free entry.

## 2. Wat Chiang Man — The City's First Temple

A short walk south brings you to Wat Chiang Man, founded in 1296 by King Mengrai when he established Chiang Mai as his new capital. The temple houses two highly revered Buddha images: the crystal Phra Setang Khamani and the stone Phra Sila. Look for the elephant-buttressed chedi in the courtyard — it is one of the most distinctive structures in the city.

## 3. Wat Pha Lat — The Forest Temple on the Monk's Trail

From the Old City, grab a songthaew to the base of the Monk's Trail on the road up to [Doi Suthep](/city/chiang-mai/attractions/doi-suthep/). The 30-minute jungle hike to Wat Pha Lat is one of Chiang Mai's best-kept secrets. Mossy stone stupas sit beneath a thick canopy, with a gentle waterfall trickling nearby. Monks still meditate here daily. Visit in the morning, ideally after a light rain, and wear shoes with good grip. Free entry.

## 4. Wat Umong — The Tunnel Temple

Head south of the Old City to Wat Umong, a 700-year-old forest monastery famous for its underground tunnels and meditation chambers. The tunnels were originally built so monks could walk and meditate away from the heat. Above ground, a large hilltop chedi overlooks a peaceful lake where turtles and fish gather. The surrounding Suan Phutthatham park adds a serene nature walk. Free entry.

## 5. Wat Sri Suphan — The Silver Temple

End your trail at Wat Sri Suphan on Wualai Road, the only temple in Thailand built almost entirely from silver. Local silversmiths from the Wualai community created the ornate panels that cover the ordination hall. Visit in the late afternoon when the silver catches the fading light — and while you're in the neighbourhood, note that Wualai is also home to some of [Chiang Mai's best street art murals](/blog/chiang-mai-street-art-walking-guide-old-city-nimman-2026/). Note: the silver chapel is open to men only; women can admire the exterior and visit the rest of the grounds. Entry is 50 baht.

## Practical Tips

- **When to walk:** Weekday mornings beat weekends. Start before breakfast — sunrise visits feel like stepping into a different Chiang Mai.
- **What to wear:** Shoulders and knees covered at every temple. Read the full [Thailand temple etiquette and dress code guide](/blog/thailand-temple-etiquette-dress-code-guide-2026/) before you go. Bring a light scarf in your bag.
- **Transport between stops:** The Old City temples are walkable. For Wat Pha Lat and Wat Umong, use a songthaew (30 to 50 baht per ride) or rent a bicycle. See [getting around Chiang Mai](/city/chiang-mai/) for more transport tips.
- **Getting to Chiang Mai:** Arrive by overnight train from Bangkok to wake up refreshed — the [Bangkok to Chiang Mai sleeper train guide](/blog/bangkok-chiang-mai-sleeper-train-guide-2026/) covers everything you need. Alternatively, [Singapore Airlines now flies nonstop to Chiang Mai](/blog/singapore-airlines-chiang-mai-nonstop-flights-2026/).
- **Where to stay:** Check the [best hotels in Chiang Mai](/best-hotels/chiang-mai/) for options across all budgets.
- **Earthquake checks:** Following recent seismic activity, Chiang Mai temples have undergone structural inspections. Restoration work is visible at some sites but all five temples listed here are open to visitors as of March 2026.

Hungry after your walk? Refuel with a bowl of [khao soi in Chiang Mai](/blog/khao-soi-chiang-mai-guide/) — the city's signature northern noodle soup.
