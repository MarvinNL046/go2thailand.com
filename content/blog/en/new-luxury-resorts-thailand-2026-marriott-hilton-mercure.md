## A Banner Year for Thai Hospitality

Thailand's hotel pipeline is booming in 2026. Several international chains are planting flags in [Phuket](/city/phuket/), [Koh Samui](/city/koh-samui/), and [Krabi](/city/krabi/) — three destinations that already dominate most visitors' itineraries. The broader [hotel boom across Thailand](/blog/thailand-hotel-boom-2026-record-pipeline-bangkok-luxury/) sets the context: international investors are committing serious capital to Thai hospitality. Here are three standout openings worth watching if you are planning a trip this year.

## 1. JW Marriott Phuket Chalong Bay Resort and Spa

**Location:** Northern end of Chalong Bay, [Phuket](/city/phuket/) — about 10 to 15 minutes by car from Chalong Pier, the island's main jumping-off point for boats to [Phi Phi](/blog/phi-phi-islands-guide-beyond-tourist-crowds/) and other southern islands.

**The property:** This is a dual-brand development. The JW Marriott side has **189 keys**, split between 146 rooms and 43 private villas grouped in their own exclusive enclave on the northwest portion of the site. Expect two outdoor pools and direct beach access.

**Meetings and events:** Nearly 2,000 square metres of meeting and event space sit between the JW Marriott and the adjacent Courtyard by Marriott (277 keys), making it one of the largest new MICE-capable properties on the island. See the full [Phuket travel guide](/blog/phuket-travel-guide-2026/) for what else the island offers in 2026.

**Opening:** Q1 2026. Marriott's careers portal is already hiring for the property, a strong indicator that doors will open soon.

## 2. Nivata Koh Samui, Tapestry Collection by Hilton

**Location:** Taling-Ngam district on [Koh Samui](/city/koh-samui/)'s quieter southwestern tip, far from the busier Chaweng and Lamai strips.

**The property:** A boutique resort with just **78 rooms**, three dining outlets (including an all-day restaurant and two bars), a fitness centre, an outdoor swimming pool, and a spa. The Tapestry Collection brand focuses on independently spirited hotels, so expect a design-forward feel distinct from the typical Hilton experience. Also on the island: the ultra-private [Cape Fahn Hotel Koh Samui](/blog/cape-fahn-hotel-koh-samui-private-island-luxury-2026/) opened earlier in 2026 on a private island.

**Why it matters:** This will be only the second Tapestry Collection property in Thailand, signalling Hilton's bet on the boutique-luxury segment here. Hilton is also expanding in the north with [Kahavadi Chiang Rai, a Curio Collection river retreat](/blog/kahavadi-chiang-rai-curio-collection-hilton-river-retreat-2026/).

**Opening:** June 2026. Getting there is now easier than ever — [Koh Samui airport is expanding its international connections](/blog/koh-samui-airport-international-hub-bangkok-airways-2026/).

## 3. Grand Mercure Krabi Ao Nang

**Location:** Ao Nang, [Krabi](/city/krabi/) — the region's main tourist hub and gateway to [Railay Beach](/city/krabi/attractions/railay-beach/), the [Four Islands](/city/krabi/attractions/four-islands-tour/), and Hong Island.

**The property:** Details are still emerging, but the Grand Mercure brand from Accor typically targets the upscale tier with locally inspired design and F&B concepts. Ao Nang has long been dominated by mid-range accommodation, so a branded upscale option fills a genuine gap. Read the full [Krabi travel guide](/blog/krabi-travel-guide/) for beaches, day trips, and where to stay right now.

**Opening:** Scheduled for 2026; exact quarter to be confirmed.

## What This Means for Travellers

More branded competition in [Phuket](/city/phuket/), [Koh Samui](/city/koh-samui/), and [Krabi](/city/krabi/) usually translates to better introductory rates and promotions. If you are flexible on dates, the months right after a resort opens often deliver the best value as properties work to build reviews and occupancy. Sign up for each chain's loyalty programme before booking — points earned on an opening-period stay can fund future nights elsewhere in Thailand.
