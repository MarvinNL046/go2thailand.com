## A Banner Year for Thai Hospitality

Thailand's hotel pipeline is booming in 2026. Several international chains are planting flags in Phuket, Koh Samui, and Krabi — three destinations that already dominate most visitors' itineraries. Here are three standout openings worth watching if you are planning a trip this year.

## 1. JW Marriott Phuket Chalong Bay Resort and Spa

**Location:** Northern end of Chalong Bay, Phuket — about 10 to 15 minutes by car from Chalong Pier, the island's main jumping-off point for boats to Phi Phi and other southern islands.

**The property:** This is a dual-brand development. The JW Marriott side has **189 keys**, split between 146 rooms and 43 private villas grouped in their own exclusive enclave on the northwest portion of the site. Expect two outdoor pools and direct beach access.

**Meetings and events:** Nearly 2,000 square metres of meeting and event space sit between the JW Marriott and the adjacent Courtyard by Marriott (277 keys), making it one of the largest new MICE-capable properties on the island.

**Opening:** Q1 2026. Marriott's careers portal is already hiring for the property, a strong indicator that doors will open soon.

## 2. Nivata Koh Samui, Tapestry Collection by Hilton

**Location:** Taling-Ngam district on Koh Samui's quieter southwestern tip, far from the busier Chaweng and Lamai strips.

**The property:** A boutique resort with just **78 rooms**, three dining outlets (including an all-day restaurant and two bars), a fitness centre, an outdoor swimming pool, and a spa. The Tapestry Collection brand focuses on independently spirited hotels, so expect a design-forward feel distinct from the typical Hilton experience.

**Why it matters:** This will be only the second Tapestry Collection property in Thailand, signalling Hilton's bet on the boutique-luxury segment here.

**Opening:** June 2026.

## 3. Grand Mercure Krabi Ao Nang

**Location:** Ao Nang, Krabi — the region's main tourist hub and gateway to Railay Beach, the Four Islands, and Hong Island.

**The property:** Details are still emerging, but the Grand Mercure brand from Accor typically targets the upscale tier with locally inspired design and F&B concepts. Ao Nang has long been dominated by mid-range accommodation, so a branded upscale option fills a genuine gap.

**Opening:** Scheduled for 2026; exact quarter to be confirmed.

## What This Means for Travellers

More branded competition in Phuket, Koh Samui, and Krabi usually translates to better introductory rates and promotions. If you are flexible on dates, the months right after a resort opens often deliver the best value as properties work to build reviews and occupancy. Sign up for each chain's loyalty programme before booking — points earned on an opening-period stay can fund future nights elsewhere in Thailand.
