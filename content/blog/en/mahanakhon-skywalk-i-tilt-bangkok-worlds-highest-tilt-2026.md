## Bangkok's Most Heart-Pounding Viewpoint

King Power Mahanakhon has been one of Bangkok's most recognisable skyscrapers since it opened, but its newest addition raises the stakes — literally. The Mahanakhon I-Tilt is the world's first and only outdoor tilting glass platform, and it sits 296 metres above the streets of Silom.

Launched in May 2025, the I-Tilt straps you into a harness and tilts your body a full 65 degrees over the edge of the building on a glass platform. Below your feet: nothing but the [Bangkok skyline](/best-places-to-visit-thailand/) stretching to the horizon. It is not for the faint-hearted, but it is an experience unlike anything else in Southeast Asia.

## What to Expect

The I-Tilt experience is located on the 75th floor of Mahanakhon, accessed through the existing SkyWalk observation deck. After a safety briefing and harness fitting, groups are guided onto the outdoor glass platform. The tilt lasts several minutes, giving you time to take in the views — and catch your breath.

Beyond the I-Tilt itself, the SkyWalk complex offers several other experiences:

- **SkyWalk Observation Deck** — an indoor and outdoor glass-floor viewing area on floors 74 and 78, offering 360-degree [panoramic views of Bangkok](/blog/bangkok-new-rooftop-bars-2026-sato-san-sanctuary-iris/).
- **SkyRides** — a virtual reality paragliding experience simulating flight over Thai landscapes.
- **SkyVerse** — Thailand's first immersive art zoo, blending digital art with interactive installations. Bangkok has several other [immersive art experiences](/blog/cave-fantasy-mbk-center-bangkok-immersive-art-2026/) worth combining with a visit here.

## Practical Information

- **Opening hours:** Daily, 10:00–19:00. Last I-Tilt round at 18:30.
- **Availability:** The I-Tilt experience runs through 30 September 2026.
- **Tickets:** Book in advance through platforms like Klook or Tiqets to guarantee a sunset slot, which fills up fast.
- **Getting there:** BTS Chong Nonsi station. The tower is directly connected to the station via a short walkway. For a full breakdown of getting around the city, see our [Bangkok public transport guide](/blog/bangkok-public-transport-bts-mrt-tourist-guide-2026/).
- **Where to stay:** The Silom area puts you within easy walking distance. Browse [Bangkok hotels](/best-hotels/bangkok/) to find something near the BTS line.
