## A 36-Storey Swing Above the Chao Phraya

[Bangkok](/city/bangkok/) has a new landmark on its skyline. **SkyFlyers: Wings of Garudapterus** opened on **29 November 2025** at Asiatique The Riverfront and stands **135 metres tall** — equivalent to a 36-storey building. It is officially the tallest giant swing ride in the Asia-Pacific region and one of the tallest in the world.

## What Is a Garudapterus?

The ride is themed around **Garudapterus**, the first pterosaur species ever identified in Southeast Asia. The fossil was discovered in Thailand, making this a uniquely local story told on a massive scale. The attraction combines the thrill of the ride with paleontology-themed spaces, including the **Garudapterus Hatchery** where younger visitors can "hatch" their own prehistoric companion through interactive displays.

## The Ride Experience

You board alongside **23 other passengers** on a giant rotating arm that swings you high above the Chao Phraya River. The ride is smooth and secure. Evening sessions are the most popular because the panoramic views of Bangkok's skyline lit up at night are spectacular — you can see the river, [Wat Arun](/blog/wat-arun-bangkok-temple-of-dawn-guide/) and the high-rises of Sathorn and Silom from the top.

Tickets can be purchased on-site or through online platforms like Ticket2Attraction, Tiqets and Klook, often with small discounts for advance booking.

## Tips for Your Visit

- **Go in the evening** — the sunset and night views are the main draw. Aim for a ride between 18:00 and 20:00.
- **Combine with Asiatique** — the riverfront complex has [restaurants](/city/bangkok/top-10-restaurants/), shops and the Jurassic World experience, so plan a full evening.
- **Not for the faint-hearted** — the ride reaches serious heights. If you are nervous about heights, watch a round from below first.
- **Kids welcome** — the interactive Garudapterus Hatchery area is designed for [families](/blog/thailand-with-kids-family-travel-guide/) and does not require riding the swing.
