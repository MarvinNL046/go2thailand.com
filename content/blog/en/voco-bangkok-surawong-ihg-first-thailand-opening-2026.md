## IHG Brings Its Fastest-Growing Premium Brand to Bangkok

IHG Hotels & Resorts has officially opened voco Bangkok Surawong, marking the debut of the voco brand in Thailand. The 244-room property sits along Surawong Road in the heart of Bangkok's [Silom district](/blog/where-to-stay-bangkok-neighborhood-guide/#1-silom-the-business-district-with-nightlife-edge) — one of the best [areas to stay in Bangkok](/blog/where-to-stay-bangkok-neighborhood-guide/) — and is now welcoming guests.

voco is IHG's fastest-growing premium brand worldwide. Choosing [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/) as the first Thai location signals the company's confidence in the city's continued appeal to upscale leisure and business travellers alike. Bangkok was recently [named the Best City in Asia for 2026](/blog/bangkok-best-city-asia-2026-destinasian-award/) by DestinAsian readers, underlining the capital's growing global reputation.

## A Brutalist Landmark Reimagined

The building has history. It previously operated as the Tawana Hotel, one of Bangkok's well-known mid-century properties. Award-winning Thai architecture firm A49 led the redesign, preserving the original Brutalist modernism while introducing natural light, open sightlines and contemporary interior flow.

The result is a hotel that feels both familiar and fresh — geometric concrete forms sit alongside warm wood finishes and lush garden courtyards. Guests who remember the old Tawana will recognise the bones of the building, but the experience is entirely new. ## Dining and Amenities

Three restaurants and bars anchor the food and drink offering:

- **Tasca Sabio** — a Spanish-inspired restaurant bringing Iberian flavours to Surawong Road
- **Deckles Smokehouse** — an all-day dining venue built around a premium smokehouse concept, a first for a Bangkok hotel of this category

Other amenities include a full lap pool surrounded by landscaped gardens, a 24-hour fitness centre and an onsen-style spa.

A small detail worth mentioning: every guest receives a [mango sticky rice](/blog/mango-sticky-rice-season-thailand/) cookie at check-in. The hotel's pastry chef prepares them daily using fresh local mangoes — a modern nod to one of Thailand's most beloved desserts. ## Location

Surawong Road puts guests within walking distance of three distinct Bangkok neighbourhoods. Silom's [street food](/blog/best-street-food-markets-bangkok/) and [nightlife](/blog/bangkok-best-cocktail-bars-march-2026-nightlife/) are steps away. Sathorn's corporate towers are a short taxi ride south. And the [BTS Skytrain](/blog/bangkok-public-transport-bts-mrt-tourist-guide-2026/) connects the area to Siam's shopping malls in under ten minutes. The [best rooftop bars in Bangkok](/blog/bangkok-new-rooftop-bars-2026-sato-san-sanctuary-iris/) are also within easy reach, and [Wat Pho](/blog/wat-pho-bangkok-reclining-buddha/) and [Wat Arun](/blog/wat-arun-bangkok-temple-of-dawn-guide/) are a short taxi or boat ride away along the Chao Phraya.
