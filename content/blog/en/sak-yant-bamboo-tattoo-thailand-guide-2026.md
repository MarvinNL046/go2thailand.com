## What Is a Sak Yant?

A Sak Yant is a sacred tattoo tradition rooted in Southeast Asian Buddhism. The name translates roughly to "blessed tattoo" in Thai. Unlike a regular tattoo parlour experience, a Sak Yant is performed by a Buddhist monk or a trained lay master called an ajarn, who uses a long bamboo rod tipped with a steel needle to hand-poke the design into the skin. The ritual is accompanied by chanting and a blessing believed to imbue the tattoo with protective or lucky powers.

The practice has deep roots in Thailand, Cambodia, Laos and Myanmar, and remains a living tradition rather than a tourist novelty. Angelina Jolie famously received a Sak Yant from the legendary Ajarn Noo Kanpai, which brought global attention to the art form.

## Most Popular Designs

Three Sak Yant designs dominate among both Thai locals and visiting travellers:

- **Hah Taew (Five Lines)** — five horizontal lines usually placed on the upper back or shoulder blade. Each line represents a different blessing, covering good fortune, protection, charm, success and warding off bad spirits.
- **Gao Yord (Nine Peaks)** — a geometric grid of nine spires representing the nine peaks of Mount Meru in Buddhist cosmology. It is one of the most universal protective designs.
- **Paed Tidt (Eight Directions)** — a circular design with eight mantras radiating outward, intended to offer protection no matter which direction you face.

Your monk or ajarn may also recommend a specific design based on a short conversation about your life and intentions.

## Monk vs Ajarn: Which Should You Choose?

Getting a Sak Yant at a temple from a monk is considered the most spiritually authentic route, but it comes with trade-offs. Monks generally choose the design for you, and hygiene standards can vary — the same ink well and steel tip may be shared between recipients, with only a quick alcohol wipe between sessions.

An ajarn master, on the other hand, typically operates from a private studio. They will discuss your preferred design, use a fresh steel tip for every client and change the ink. Prices at an ajarn studio in Bangkok start from around 2,500 baht, while Chiang Mai studios begin at approximately 2,000 baht.

## Healing and Aftercare

One practical advantage of the bamboo method is faster healing. Because the hand-poke technique causes less trauma to the skin than a machine tattoo, there is less scabbing and the healing period is typically just two to four days, compared to four to seven days for a machine tattoo. Keep the area clean, avoid swimming pools for a few days and follow any aftercare advice your artist provides.

## Where to Get One in 2026

Chiang Mai is the most popular city for Sak Yant among travellers. Dozens of reputable studios and temples offer the service, and the relaxed northern atmosphere suits the meditative nature of the ritual. Bangkok is another strong option, with well-known ajarn studios offering higher-end experiences. Ayutthaya, the former royal capital, is a less crowded alternative with strong spiritual heritage.

Wherever you go, do your research, read recent reviews, and make sure the studio uses fresh needles. A Sak Yant is permanent — take the time to get it done right.
