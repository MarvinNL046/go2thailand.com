## Thailand Makes Wine — And It Is Better Than You Think

Most visitors do not associate Thailand with wine, but roughly 160 kilometres northeast of Bangkok lies Khao Yai — a region that has quietly been producing award-winning bottles for over two decades. The Khao Yai Food & Wine Festival 2026 shines a spotlight on this unexpected side of Thai tourism, combining vineyard tours with regional gastronomy and Korat GI (Geographical Indication) heritage products from [Nakhon Ratchasima Province](/city/nakhon-ratchasima/). It is one of the most compelling [best day trips from Bangkok](/blog/best-day-trips-from-bangkok/) for food lovers in 2026.

## The Wineries Worth Visiting

### PB Valley Khao Yai Winery

PB Valley is the birthplace of the Khao Yai wine region. The estate sprawls across more than 800 acres at the edge of [Khao Yai National Park](/city/nakhon-ratchasima/attractions/khao-yai-national-park/), sitting between 300 and 380 metres above sea level. Shiraz, Tempranillo and Chenin Blanc are among its best-known varieties. Visitors can hop on a tram tour through the vineyards, learn about the production process and finish with a guided tasting. For a deeper look at what else the park offers, read our dedicated [Khao Yai National Park day trip guide](/blog/khao-yai-national-park-day-trip-guide-2026/).

### GranMonte Estate

Located in the Asoke Valley adjacent to the national park, GranMonte was established in 1999 on a former cashew plantation. It has earned a reputation for producing some of Thailand's most critically acclaimed wines. The estate also operates a restaurant where dishes are paired with its own vintages — a worthwhile stop for food-and-wine enthusiasts. Thailand's restaurant scene is riding high right now, as the [Michelin Guide Thailand 2026](/blog/thailand-michelin-guide-2026-new-stars-suhring/) and the growing list of [Bib Gourmand street food picks](/blog/thailand-bib-gourmand-2026-new-restaurants-street-food/) both show.

## Korat GI Products

The 2026 festival goes beyond wine by showcasing products that carry a Geographical Indication label from the [Nakhon Ratchasima](/destinations/nakhon-ratchasima/) area. GI certification means the product's quality or reputation is linked to its place of origin, similar to Champagne in France or Parma ham in Italy. Expect local cheeses, honey, dried fruits and artisanal sauces alongside the wines. Thailand's culinary identity runs deep — the [Thailand Tourism Festival culinary guide](/blog/thailand-tourism-festival-2026-culinary-regional-food-guide/) explores regional food traditions across every corner of the country.

## Best Time to Visit the Vineyards

Grape harvest season in Khao Yai typically runs from February to March, making right now one of the best windows for a visit. During harvest, wineries offer grape-picking experiences and special tasting events that are not available the rest of the year. The cooler months from November to February are also excellent, with daytime temperatures hovering around 20-25 degrees Celsius. For a full overview of when to visit different parts of Thailand, see the [best time to visit Thailand guide](/blog/best-time-to-visit-thailand/).

## How to Get There from Bangkok

- **By car** — the drive takes about 2.5 to 3 hours via Motorway 6 (Bangkok-Nakhon Ratchasima). This is the most flexible option if you plan to visit multiple vineyards. Nakhon Ratchasima — known as Korat — is the [regional hub for the area](/city/nakhon-ratchasima/).
- **By tour** — several operators on Klook and GetYourGuide run full-day vineyard tours from Bangkok, typically including transport, tastings and lunch. Prices start around 2,500 baht per person. Check the [Bangkok public transport guide](/blog/bangkok-public-transport-bts-mrt-tourist-guide-2026/) if you need to get across Bangkok first.
- **By bus** — public buses depart from Mo Chit Bus Terminal to Pak Chong, the gateway town for Khao Yai. From there you will need a songthaew or taxi to reach the wineries.

## Practical Tips

- **Do not drive after tasting.** If you plan to sample wines at multiple estates, arrange a designated driver or book a tour.
- **Combine with the national park.** Khao Yai National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with waterfalls, hiking trails and wild elephants. A two-day trip covering both the park and the vineyards is ideal. The park is also home to [ethical wildlife encounters](/blog/ethical-elephant-sanctuaries-thailand-2026-guide/) worth knowing about.
- **Book winery visits in advance.** Guided tours and tasting sessions sometimes require reservations, especially during festival weekends.
- **Check your visa status.** Make sure you have the right entry permit sorted before travelling — the [Thailand visa guide 2026](/blog/thailand-visa-guide-2026/) covers all current categories.
