## Thailand Makes Wine — And It Is Better Than You Think

Most visitors do not associate Thailand with wine, but roughly 160 kilometres northeast of Bangkok lies Khao Yai — a region that has quietly been producing award-winning bottles for over two decades. The Khao Yai Food & Wine Festival 2026 shines a spotlight on this unexpected side of Thai tourism, combining vineyard tours with regional gastronomy and Korat GI (Geographical Indication) heritage products from Nakhon Ratchasima Province.

## The Wineries Worth Visiting

### PB Valley Khao Yai Winery

PB Valley is the birthplace of the Khao Yai wine region. The estate sprawls across more than 800 acres at the edge of Khao Yai National Park, sitting between 300 and 380 metres above sea level. Shiraz, Tempranillo and Chenin Blanc are among its best-known varieties. Visitors can hop on a tram tour through the vineyards, learn about the production process and finish with a guided tasting.

### GranMonte Estate

Located in the Asoke Valley adjacent to the national park, GranMonte was established in 1999 on a former cashew plantation. It has earned a reputation for producing some of Thailand's most critically acclaimed wines. The estate also operates a restaurant where dishes are paired with its own vintages — a worthwhile stop for food-and-wine enthusiasts.

## Korat GI Products

The 2026 festival goes beyond wine by showcasing products that carry a Geographical Indication label from the Nakhon Ratchasima area. GI certification means the product's quality or reputation is linked to its place of origin, similar to Champagne in France or Parma ham in Italy. Expect local cheeses, honey, dried fruits and artisanal sauces alongside the wines.

## Best Time to Visit the Vineyards

Grape harvest season in Khao Yai typically runs from February to March, making right now one of the best windows for a visit. During harvest, wineries offer grape-picking experiences and special tasting events that are not available the rest of the year. The cooler months from November to February are also excellent, with daytime temperatures hovering around 20-25 degrees Celsius.

## How to Get There from Bangkok

- **By car** — the drive takes about 2.5 to 3 hours via Motorway 6 (Bangkok-Nakhon Ratchasima). This is the most flexible option if you plan to visit multiple vineyards.
- **By tour** — several operators on Klook and GetYourGuide run full-day vineyard tours from Bangkok, typically including transport, tastings and lunch. Prices start around 2,500 baht per person.
- **By bus** — public buses depart from Mo Chit Bus Terminal to Pak Chong, the gateway town for Khao Yai. From there you will need a songthaew or taxi to reach the wineries.

## Practical Tips

- **Do not drive after tasting.** If you plan to sample wines at multiple estates, arrange a designated driver or book a tour.
- **Combine with the national park.** Khao Yai National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with waterfalls, hiking trails and wild elephants. A two-day trip covering both the park and the vineyards is ideal.
- **Book winery visits in advance.** Guided tours and tasting sessions sometimes require reservations, especially during festival weekends.

Khao Yai proves that Thailand's food scene extends far beyond street food and curries. A vineyard day trip is a refreshing change of pace — and the wines might genuinely surprise you.
