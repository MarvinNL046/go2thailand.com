## Electronic Music Legends Come to Bangkok

Kraftwerk, the German group widely credited with shaping electronic music as we know it, will perform in Bangkok on Sunday, May 10, 2026. It is the first time the band has ever played in Thailand, and one of only two Southeast Asian stops on their current Multimedia World Tour.

The show takes place at the Queen Sirikit National Convention Center (QSNCC) in the Khlong Toei district of Bangkok — easily reachable via the MRT (Queen Sirikit National Convention Center station).

## What Is the Multimedia Tour?

Kraftwerk's live shows are not standard concerts. The group performs behind matching consoles while a full-venue 3D visual system projects animations, geometric patterns, and archival footage in sync with the music. Sound, light, and video operate as a single precisely engineered system, turning the audience space into an immersive audiovisual environment.

The setlist typically draws from more than four decades of material, including tracks from Autobahn, The Man-Machine, Computer World, and Tour de France.

## Tickets and Prices

Tickets are available through Thai Ticket Major. Three pricing tiers have been announced:

- **Standard:** 3,300 baht
- **Premium:** 3,800 baht
- **VIP:** 5,500 baht

Tickets went on sale in November 2025 and have been selling steadily, so check availability early if you plan to attend.

## Why It Matters for Bangkok

Bangkok's live music scene has grown rapidly over the past few years, with major international acts increasingly adding the city to their Asian tour routes. Kraftwerk's debut here follows a trend that has already seen K-pop groups, EDM headliners, and legacy rock bands choosing QSNCC and other Bangkok venues over more traditional stops like Tokyo or Singapore.

For travellers visiting Thailand in May 2026, the concert offers a strong reason to spend an extra night in Bangkok before heading to the islands or the north.

## Practical Tips

- **Get there early.** QSNCC doors typically open 60 to 90 minutes before showtime.
- **Use the MRT.** The venue sits directly above Queen Sirikit MRT station on the Blue Line — no taxi needed.
- **Combine with dinner.** The nearby Benjakitti Park area and Sukhumvit Soi 16 have plenty of restaurant options for a pre-show meal.

This is a rare chance to see one of the most influential groups in music history perform in Thailand for the first time.
