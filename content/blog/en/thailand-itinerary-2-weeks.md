Two weeks in Thailand sounds like a dream—and it absolutely can be. But without a solid plan, you'll waste precious days figuring out logistics instead of experiencing the magic. **A Thailand itinerary 2 weeks** is the sweet spot: long enough to escape the tourist treadmill, short enough to stay focused and energized.

We've spent years living in and traveling through Thailand, and we've tested this exact route with dozens of travelers. Here's what works.

## Key Takeaways

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **What is the best time to visit?** | November–February (cool, dry season). Avoid May–September (monsoon). |
| **How much does it cost?** | Budget: ฿25,000–35,000 total ($700–980 USD) for 2 weeks. Mid-range: ฿45,000–60,000 total ($1,250–1,670 USD) for 2 weeks. |
| **How do I get there?** | Fly into [Bangkok](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/) (Suvarnabhumi Airport). Domestic flights or buses connect to other cities. |
| **Is it safe?** | Yes. Thailand is generally safe for tourists. Avoid political gatherings and use common sense with valuables. |
| **What should I book in advance?** | Flights, accommodation in peak season, cooking classes, and long-distance buses/trains. |
| **Do I need a visa?** | Most nationalities get 60 days visa-free. Check [Thailand visa requirements](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/) for your country. |
| **How much time per city?** | Bangkok (3 days), [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) (3–4 days), islands (5–6 days). |

## 1. Pre-Trip Essentials: Visa, Insurance & Documentation

Before you book a single flight, handle the administrative basics. Most Western nationalities (US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia) qualify for **60-day visa-free entry** to Thailand. You'll need a valid passport with at least 6 months validity from your arrival date.

Upon arrival, you'll be required to complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) online at least 3 days before your flight. This replaced the old TM.6 form and takes about 5 minutes. You'll receive a QR code to scan at immigration—no paper required.

**Travel insurance is non-negotiable.** Thailand has excellent hospitals, but costs add up fast. A decent policy covering medical evacuation, trip cancellation, and lost baggage runs ฿3,000–5,000 ($85–140 USD) for two weeks. We recommend checking [travel insurance options](https://go2-thailand.com/travel-insurance-thailand/) before departure.

## 2. Days 1–3: Bangkok — The Chaotic Heart

**Bangkok is unavoidable, and honestly, you shouldn't avoid it.** This city of 10 million pulses with energy, street food, temples, and chaos in equal measure. Three days gives you time to adjust to the heat and humidity without getting overwhelmed.

### Day 1: Arrival & Riverside

Arrive at Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK), 25 km east of central Bangkok. The Airport Rail Link train (฿45/$1.25 USD) takes 28 minutes to Phaya Thai station in the city center—faster and cheaper than taxis. From there, a short taxi or Grab ride (฿100–150/$3–4 USD) gets you to your hotel.

Spend your first afternoon recovering from the flight. Check into your accommodation, grab [Thai iced tea](https://go2-thailand.com/drinks/thai-iced-tea/) at a 7-Eleven, and take a walk around your neighborhood. We recommend staying in **Sukhumvit, Silom, or Rattanakosin** areas—all have good transport links and nightlife.

In the evening, head to the riverside (Chao Phraya River) for sunset. The views are stunning, and the breeze offers relief from the heat.

### Day 2: Temples & Markets

Start early at **Wat Phra Kaew** (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) and the adjacent Grand Palace. Entry is ฿500 ($14 USD). Dress respectfully: covered shoulders and knees required. The temple is jaw-dropping, but it gets crowded by 10 a.m.—arrive by 8:30 a.m.

Next, visit **Wat Pho** (Temple of the Reclining Buddha), just south across the street. Entry is ฿200 ($5.50 USD). The 46-meter gold-plated reclining Buddha is one of Thailand's most iconic sights.

Lunch at a local khao man gai (chicken rice) stall nearby—expect to pay ฿40–60 ($1–2 USD) for a full meal. In the afternoon, explore **Chatuchak Market** (if it's the weekend) or **Talad Noi** (a chaotic old market with narrow alleys, street food, and antiques). Both are free to wander.

Evening: Try [Bangkok street food](https://go2-thailand.com/blog/bangkok-street-food-beginners/) at a night market. **Talad Rod Fai** (Train Market) or **Jodd Fairs** are popular. Expect ฿150–300 ($4–8 USD) for a feast.

### Day 3: Culture & Nightlife

Visit **Jim Thompson House**, a museum dedicated to the American silk entrepreneur who mysteriously disappeared in 1967. Guided tours (฿200/$5.50 USD) run every 20 minutes and reveal fascinating Thai architecture and history.

Spend the afternoon at **Lumphini Park**, Bangkok's largest public park. It's peaceful, tree-lined, and locals exercise here at dawn and dusk. Entry is free.

For dinner, try a rooftop bar with Chao Phraya views, or head to **Chinatown (Yaowarat)** for authentic street food. [Pad Thai](https://go2-thailand.com/food/pad-thai/), [som tam](https://go2-thailand.com/food/som-tam/), and [mango sticky rice](https://go2-thailand.com/food/mango-sticky-rice/) are must-tries.

## 3. Days 4–5: Chiang Mai — Mountains & Temples

From Bangkok, you have three transport options to [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) (720 km north):

| Option | Duration | Cost (THB) | Best For |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Overnight train** | 11–13 hours | 600–1,500 | Comfort, [experience](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK), saves a hotel night |
| **Domestic flight** | 1.5 hours | 1,200–2,500 | Speed, less tiring |
| **Bus** | 9–10 hours | 400–800 | Budget travelers, scenic route |

We recommend the **overnight train** (departing Bangkok ~7 p.m., arriving Chiang Mai ~7 a.m.). It's an experience in itself, and you save a night's accommodation. Book through [12Go Asia](https://12go.tpo.lv/tNA80urD) or at [Hua Lamphong](https://trip.tpo.lv/gNIdNBmi) Station.

Chiang Mai's old city is surrounded by a moat and ancient walls. It's walkable, slower-paced than Bangkok, and genuinely charming. Stay in the **Nimmanhaemin** or **Old City** areas.

Visit **Wat Chedi Luang**, a massive 15th-century temple with a partially collapsed chedi (stupa). Entry is ฿40 ($1 USD). Next, explore **Wat Phra Singh**, known for its intricate wood carvings.

In the afternoon, take a **[cooking class](https://klook.tpo.lv/7Dt6WApj)**. This is one of Thailand's best experiences. Classes run 4–5 hours, include a market tour, and cost ฿800–1,500 ($22–42 USD). You'll learn to make [green curry](https://go2-thailand.com/food/green-curry/), [pad thai](https://go2-thailand.com/food/pad-thai/), and other classics. Book through your hotel or [Klook](https://klook.tpo.lv/7Dt6WApj).

Evening: Stroll the **Sunday Walking Street Market** (if you're there on a Sunday) or grab dinner at a local restaurant. [Khao soi](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/food/) (curry noodle soup) is Chiang Mai's signature dish—try it everywhere.

### Day 5: Elephant Sanctuary or Trekking

This is where many travelers make a mistake: visiting unethical elephant camps. **Avoid riding elephants.** Instead, visit a legitimate **elephant sanctuary** where rescued elephants live in natural conditions. Sanctuaries like Elephant Nature Park offer full-day experiences (฿2,500–3,500/$70–100 USD) where you observe, feed, and bathe elephants ethically.

Alternatively, take a **jungle trek** with a local guide. Half-day treks (฿600–1,000/$17–28 USD) include hiking, waterfall visits, and sometimes village homestays. Book through your hotel or [GetYourGuide](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK).

## 4. Days 6–7: Chiang Rai & Golden Triangle (Optional Detour)

If you have time, take a day trip or overnight to **Chiang Rai** (3 hours north). The **White Temple (Wat Rong Khun)** is Instagram-famous and genuinely stunning—a modern, all-white Buddhist temple with surreal architecture. Entry is ฿50 ($1.40 USD).

Nearby is the **Golden Triangle**, where Thailand, Laos, and Myanmar meet. You can visit the **Opium Museum** (฿200/$5.50 USD) and take a boat on the Mekong River.

**Alternatively**, skip Chiang Rai and spend the extra time island-hopping or relaxing. Two weeks moves fast—don't overpack your itinerary.

## 5. Days 8–9: Transport to Southern Islands

From Chiang Mai, fly back to Bangkok (1.5 hours, ฿1,200–2,000/$34–56 USD), then fly south to your island destination. Options:

- **[Koh Samui](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-samui/)** — Most developed, best infrastructure, pricier
- **[Koh Phangan](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phangan/)** — Younger crowd, Full Moon Party, good beaches
- **[Koh Tao](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-tao/)** — Best for diving, budget-friendly, small
- **[Krabi](https://go2-thailand.com/transport/bangkok-to-krabi/)** — Mainland base for island hopping, stunning limestone cliffs

**Our recommendation:** Fly to **Krabi** (4 hours from Bangkok, ฿1,500–2,500/$42–70 USD). From there, take ferries to nearby islands like [Koh Phi Phi](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phi-phi/) (45 minutes, ฿400/$11 USD) or [Koh Lanta](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-lanta/) (2 hours, ฿300/$8.50 USD).

### Day 8: Krabi Town & Railay Beach

Spend a night in Krabi town to break up travel. Explore the **Krabi Town Night Market** (free, excellent food), or head straight to **Railay Beach** via longtail boat (฿100/$3 USD from Ao Nang Beach, 20 minutes).

Railay is stunning—towering limestone cliffs, turquoise water, and a car-free beach. It's touristy but worth it. Accommodation ranges from ฿400–2,000+ ($11–56+ USD) per night.

### Day 9: Island Hopping & Snorkeling

Take a **4 Islands tour** from Krabi (฿600–1,200/$17–34 USD). You'll visit Koh Chicken, Koh Phra Nang, Bamboo Island, and Phra Nang Cave Beach. Snorkeling is decent, and the scenery is postcard-perfect.

Alternatively, take a ferry to [Koh Phi Phi](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phi-phi/) and spend the night. It's more crowded but has better nightlife and diving.

## 6. Days 10–12: Island Relaxation & Water Activities

Pick one or two islands and settle in. This is your beach time.

**Best activities:**

- **Diving & Snorkeling:** [Koh Tao](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-tao/) is the diving capital. PADI Open Water courses cost ฿8,000–10,000 ($225–280 USD). Day dives run ฿1,500–2,500 ($42–70 USD).
- **Rock Climbing:** Railay Beach and Ton Sai Beach (Krabi) have world-class climbing. Guided climbs cost ฿1,500–2,500 ($42–70 USD).
- **Kayaking:** Explore mangrove forests and limestone caves. Half-day tours: ฿800–1,500 ($22–42 USD).
- **Spa & Massage:** Thai massage costs ฿200–400 ($5.50–11 USD) per hour. Beachside spas are magical at sunset.

**Food on the islands:**

Expect to pay 20–30% more than mainland Thailand. A meal at a tourist restaurant: ฿250–400 ($7–11 USD). Local stalls: ฿60–120 ($1.70–3.40 USD).

Try **fresh seafood grilled on the beach**, [tom yum goong](https://go2-thailand.com/food/tom-yum-goong/) (spicy shrimp soup), and tropical fruit smoothies.

## 7. Days 13–14: Return to Bangkok or Extend

With two days left, you have options:

**Option A: Return to Bangkok**
Fly back (4 hours, ฿1,500–2,500/$42–70 USD) and spend your final days exploring neighborhoods you missed—**Thonglor** (upscale dining), **Ari** (local vibe), or **Banglamphu** (backpacker hub with street food).

**Option B: Extend Your Island Time**
If you're not ready to leave, stay put. Relax, dive again, or take a cooking class on the island.

**Option C: Visit Phuket**
[Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/city/phuket/) is Thailand's largest island and a major hub. It's more developed but less charming than smaller islands. Good for nightlife, shopping, and water sports.

## 9. Practical Tips: Transport, Money & Connectivity

**Getting Around:**
- **Bangkok:** BTS Skytrain and MRT subway are fast, cheap (฿15–65/$0.40–1.80 USD), and air-conditioned. Grab (ride-hailing app) costs ฿50–150 ($1.40–4.25 USD) per ride.
- **Between Cities:** Overnight trains and buses are comfortable and save hotel costs. Book 2–3 days ahead.
- **Islands:** Ferries and longtail boats are the main transport. Prices vary by route (฿200–500/$5.50–14 USD).

**Money:**
- Thai Baht (THB) is the currency. ATMs are everywhere. Withdraw ฿10,000–20,000 at a time to minimize fees.
- Credit cards work in cities but bring cash for islands and small towns.
- Exchange rates: 1 USD ≈ 35 THB (as of Feb 2026).

**Staying Connected:**
Get a local SIM card or [eSIM](https://go2-thailand.com/esim/) for data. Options:
- **AIS, DTAC, True Move:** ฿99–299 ($2.80–8.50 USD) for 7-day plans with 4–8 GB data.
- **eSIM:** More convenient, similar pricing. Buy before you arrive or at the airport.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best time to visit Thailand?

**November to February** is ideal—cool, dry, and sunny. Avoid May–September (monsoon season, humidity, occasional flooding). March–April is hot and crowded.

### Do I need to book everything in advance?

Not everything, but **book flights, accommodation in peak season, and long-distance transport 2–4 weeks ahead.** Cooking classes and tours can be booked 1–2 days before. Islands and small towns have [last-minute](https://booking.tpo.lv/pDNjHJA1) availability.

### Is Thailand expensive?

No. Thailand is one of Southeast Asia's most affordable destinations. Budget travelers spend ฿1,500–2,000/day ($42–56 USD). Mid-range travelers spend ฿3,000–4,500/day ($85–130 USD).

### Can I extend my stay beyond 60 days?

Yes. You can apply for a **visa extension** (add 30 days) at any Immigration Office for ฿1,900 ($54 USD). Or leave and re-enter Thailand to reset your visa-free period (not recommended—immigration may question this).

### What if I get sick?

Thailand has excellent hospitals, especially in Bangkok and Chiang Mai. **Bangkok Hospital** and **Bumrungrad International Hospital** are world-class. Travel insurance covers most costs. Minor issues: visit a 7-Eleven pharmacy (pharmacists speak English).

### Is it safe to travel alone?

Yes. Thailand is generally safe. Avoid displaying expensive items, don't accept drinks from strangers, and use registered taxis or Grab. Women travelers should take standard precautions (avoid walking alone late at night, etc.). Check [Thailand safety guide](https://go2-thailand.com/blog/is-thailand-safe-tourists-2026/) for current conditions.
