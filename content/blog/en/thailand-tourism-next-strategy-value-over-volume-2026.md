## "Value is the New Volume"

The Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) has unveiled its strategic direction for 2026 under the theme "Value is the New Volume." This marks a significant pivot away from chasing raw visitor numbers toward higher-quality, value-led tourism that balances economic growth with social and environmental sustainability.

The ambition is substantial: TAT is targeting 3 trillion baht in total tourism revenue for 2026, with each visitor expected to contribute more through richer, more meaningful travel experiences.

## What Is "Tourism Next"?

Thailand Tourism Next is the overarching framework guiding the country's tourism development this year. Its core pillars include:

- **Quality over quantity** — attracting visitors who spend more and stay longer rather than maximizing headcount
- **Sustainability** — protecting natural and cultural assets while growing the economy
- **Emotional storytelling** — promoting experiences that reflect authentic Thai identity and create lasting memories
- **Community-based tourism** — channeling visitors toward secondary cities and lesser-known destinations

## TAGTHAi Goes Green

A key player in the new strategy is TAGTHAi, Thailand's digital tourism platform. Previously known primarily as a convenience app for booking attractions and transport, TAGTHAi is evolving into a cornerstone of sustainable tourism under the Tourism Next framework.

The platform has launched its EcoVenture programme, starting with a pilot marine education initiative that combines youth learning, environmental awareness, and sustainable tourism development. TAGTHAi is also promoting green hotels and responsible tour operators through its platform.

## Focus on Hidden Gems

One of the most exciting aspects of the 2026 strategy is the push to promote community-based tourism in Thailand's secondary cities. Rather than funneling all visitors through Bangkok, Phuket, and Chiang Mai, the TAT wants travelers to discover the "hidden gems" that make up the country's diverse cultural landscape.

The Thai Ecotourism and Adventure Travel Association (TEATA) plays a central role here, providing a national framework that defines what sustainable tourism in Thailand truly means — going beyond simple green labels.

## What This Means for Travelers

For visitors planning a trip to Thailand in 2026, the Tourism Next strategy translates into more curated, authentic experiences across a wider range of destinations. Expect to see more community homestays, eco-tourism packages, and cultural immersion programs promoted alongside the traditional beach and city offerings. Thailand is betting that travelers want depth, not just distance — and the country is reshaping itself accordingly.
