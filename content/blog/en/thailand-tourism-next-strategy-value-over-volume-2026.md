## "Value is the New Volume"

The Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) has unveiled its strategic direction for 2026 under the theme "Value is the New Volume." This marks a significant pivot away from chasing raw visitor numbers toward higher-quality, value-led tourism that balances economic growth with social and environmental sustainability. This strategy sits alongside the broader [10% growth recovery target Thailand is pursuing in 2026](/blog/thailand-tourism-growth-target-2026-recovery-after-2025-decline/).

The ambition is substantial: TAT is targeting [3 trillion baht in total tourism revenue for 2026](/blog/thailand-3-trillion-baht-tourism-target-amazing-5-economy-2026/), with each visitor expected to contribute more through richer, more meaningful travel experiences.

## What Is "Tourism Next"?

Thailand Tourism Next is the overarching framework guiding the country's tourism development this year. Its core pillars include:

- **Quality over quantity** — attracting visitors who spend more and stay longer rather than maximizing headcount, a theme also explored in [Pattaya's own quality-over-quantity paradox](/blog/pattaya-tourism-paradox-quality-over-quantity-2026/)
- **Sustainability** — protecting natural and cultural assets while growing the economy; see [Thailand's Good Travel certification programme](/blog/thailand-good-travel-certification-41-sustainable-destinations-2026/) for how this works in practice
- **Emotional storytelling** — promoting experiences that reflect authentic Thai identity and create lasting memories
- **Community-based tourism** — channeling visitors toward secondary cities and lesser-known destinations, as also highlighted in [Thailand Shifts Gears: Fewer Visitors, Better Experiences](/blog/thailand-tourism-shifts-gears-fewer-visitors-better-experiences-2026/)

## TAGTHAi Goes Green

A key player in the new strategy is [TAGTHAi](/blog/tagthai-app-sustainable-travel-guide-2026/), Thailand's digital tourism platform. Previously known primarily as a convenience app for booking attractions and transport, TAGTHAi is evolving into a cornerstone of sustainable tourism under the Tourism Next framework. It works in parallel with the [Amazing Thailand App](/blog/amazing-thailand-app-ai-mastercard-2026/), which uses AI to personalise travel recommendations.

The platform has launched its EcoVenture programme, starting with a pilot marine education initiative that combines youth learning, environmental awareness, and sustainable tourism development. TAGTHAi is also promoting [green hotels](/blog/thailand-star-green-tourism-accreditation-2026/) and responsible tour operators through its platform.

## Focus on Hidden Gems

One of the most exciting aspects of the 2026 strategy is the push to promote community-based tourism in Thailand's secondary cities. Rather than funneling all visitors through [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/), [Phuket](/city/phuket/), and [Chiang Mai](/city/chiang-mai/), the TAT wants travelers to discover the [hidden gems that most tourists miss](/blog/15-hidden-gems-thailand-tourists-miss/). The [Mekong riverside provinces](/blog/thailand-mekong-riverside-seven-provinces-sustainable-tourism-2026/) are one example of this decentralised approach in action.

The Thai Ecotourism and Adventure Travel Association (TEATA) plays a central role here, providing a national framework that defines what sustainable tourism in Thailand truly means — going beyond simple green labels. This is also a key theme in [The New Thailand presentation at ITB Berlin 2026](/blog/the-new-thailand-itb-berlin-2026-sustainable-wellness-tourism/).

## What This Means for Travelers

For visitors planning a trip to Thailand in 2026, the Tourism Next strategy translates into more curated, authentic experiences across a wider range of destinations. Expect to see more community homestays, eco-tourism packages, and cultural immersion programs promoted alongside the traditional beach and city offerings. Thailand is betting that travelers want depth, not just distance — and the country is reshaping itself accordingly.
