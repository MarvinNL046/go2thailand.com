## Thailand's Biggest Light Festival Runs Until May 3

If you are looking for something completely different from beaches and temples, the Nasatta Light Festival 2026 is one of the most striking events in Thailand right now. Running nightly until May 3, 2026 at Nasatta Park in Bang Phae district, Ratchaburi province, the festival transforms more than 30 rai (roughly 12 acres) of woodland into an illuminated fairy-tale landscape.

The theme for this edition is "Fairy Dreams," drawing on Thai mythology, regional heritage, and sustainable design to create a nighttime experience unlike anything else in the country.

## What You Will See

The festival is divided into 24 themed zones spread across the park's forested grounds. Highlights include illuminated tunnels called "Breath of Light," floating lantern displays inspired by Loi Krathong, and large-scale sculptural floral installations that reinterpret creatures and spirits from Thai folklore.

Several zones are interactive. Visitors can walk through glowing light forests, write wishes beneath illuminated trees, and step into specially designed photo spots built for dramatic night photography. The combination of natural woodland and carefully placed lighting creates a genuinely immersive atmosphere — this is not just a few LED panels in a field.

Sustainability is woven into the production. The organisers use energy-efficient lighting throughout and have incorporated repurposed materials into many of the installations.

## Practical Information

- **Dates:** Now through May 3, 2026
- **Hours:** 6:00 PM to 10:00 PM (ticket sales close at 9:30 PM), Tuesday to Sunday and public holidays. Closed on Mondays.
- **Location:** Nasatta Park, Bang Phae district, Ratchaburi — approximately 90 minutes west of Bangkok by car.
- **Tickets:** Available on-site and through the Nasatta Light Festival website. Check current pricing at [nasattalightfestival.com](https://www.nasattalightfestival.com/en/nasatta-light-festival-product-ticket/).

## Getting There from Bangkok

The most practical option is to drive or hire a car. Take the Phetkasem Road (Highway 4) west toward Ratchaburi, then follow signs to Bang Phae and Nasatta Park. There is parking on-site.

For travellers without a car, several tour operators in Bangkok offer evening packages that include transport, admission, and dinner at a local restaurant.

## Who Is It For?

The Nasatta Light Festival works well for families, couples, and photographers. The park setting is flat and walkable, though comfortable shoes are recommended for the unpaved forest paths. Evenings can still be warm in March and April, so dress lightly and bring water.

With only a few weeks left before the festival closes on May 3, this is one of the most memorable evening activities available near Bangkok right now.
