## Thailand's Biggest Light Festival Runs Until May 3

If you are looking for something completely different from beaches and temples, the Nasatta Light Festival 2026 is one of the most striking events in Thailand right now. Running nightly until May 3, 2026 at Nasatta Park in Bang Phae district, Ratchaburi province, the festival transforms more than 30 rai (roughly 12 acres) of woodland into an illuminated fairy-tale landscape. It sits firmly among the [best day trips from Bangkok](/blog/best-day-trips-from-bangkok/) this season.

The theme for this edition is "Fairy Dreams," drawing on Thai mythology, regional heritage, and sustainable design to create a nighttime experience unlike anything else in the country.

## What You Will See

The festival is divided into 24 themed zones spread across the park's forested grounds. Highlights include illuminated tunnels called "Breath of Light," floating lantern displays inspired by [Loi Krathong](/blog/loi-krathong-yi-peng-2026-festival-guide/), and large-scale sculptural floral installations that reinterpret creatures and spirits from Thai folklore.

Several zones are interactive. Visitors can walk through glowing light forests, write wishes beneath illuminated trees, and step into specially designed photo spots built for dramatic night photography. The combination of natural woodland and carefully placed lighting creates a genuinely immersive atmosphere — this is not just a few LED panels in a field.

Sustainability is woven into the production. The organisers use energy-efficient lighting throughout and have incorporated repurposed materials into many of the installations. Thailand's growing [sustainable tourism credentials](/blog/thailand-good-travel-certification-41-sustainable-destinations-2026/) make events like this increasingly common.

## Practical Information

- **Dates:** Now through May 3, 2026
- **Hours:** 6:00 PM to 10:00 PM (ticket sales close at 9:30 PM), Tuesday to Sunday and public holidays. Closed on Mondays.
- **Location:** Nasatta Park, Bang Phae district, Ratchaburi — approximately 90 minutes west of [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/) by car.
- **Tickets:** Available on-site and through the Nasatta Light Festival website. Check current pricing at [nasattalightfestival.com](https://www.nasattalightfestival.com/en/nasatta-light-festival-product-ticket/).
- **Best time to visit:** March and April evenings are warm but dry; check our [Thailand weather guide](/blog/best-time-to-visit-thailand/) for seasonal context.

## Getting There from Bangkok

The most practical option is to drive or hire a car. Take the Phetkasem Road (Highway 4) west toward Ratchaburi, then follow signs to Bang Phae and Nasatta Park. There is parking on-site.

For travellers without a car, several tour operators in [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/) offer evening packages that include transport, admission, and dinner at a local restaurant. If you are relying on public transport within the city first, the [Bangkok BTS and MRT tourist guide](/blog/bangkok-public-transport-bts-mrt-tourist-guide-2026/) explains how to get to the main bus and minivan terminals heading west.

While you are in this part of the country, the [Tha Kha floating market in Samut Songkhram](/blog/tha-kha-floating-market-samut-songkhram-hidden-gem-2026/) and the [Damnoen Saduak and Amphawa floating markets](/blog/bangkok-floating-markets-guide-damnoen-amphawa-2026/) are easy to combine into a full day out from Bangkok before the festival opens in the evening.

## Who Is It For?

The Nasatta Light Festival works well for [families](/blog/thailand-with-kids-family-travel-guide/), couples, and photographers. The park setting is flat and walkable, though comfortable shoes are recommended for the unpaved forest paths. Evenings can still be warm in March and April, so dress lightly and bring water. See [Thailand's rainy season travel tips](/blog/thailand-rainy-season-travel-tips-2026/) if your visit extends into May.
