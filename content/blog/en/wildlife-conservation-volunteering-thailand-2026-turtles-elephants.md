## Why Volunteer for Wildlife in Thailand?

Thailand is home to incredible biodiversity, from endangered sea turtles nesting on southern beaches to rescued elephants recovering in northern sanctuaries. But habitat loss, poaching and tourism pressure continue to threaten these animals. Conservation volunteering lets travellers make a real impact while experiencing a side of Thailand most tourists never see.

The good news: there are reputable, ethical programmes running year-round in 2026 that welcome volunteers with no prior experience.

## Sea Turtle and Dugong Conservation in Trang

One of the most hands-on marine programmes in Thailand is run by GVI in the southern province of Trang. Volunteers help protect endangered sea turtles and dugongs — the gentle "sea cows" that graze on seagrass meadows along the Andaman coast.

Daily activities include snorkel-based surveys, seagrass habitat monitoring, species identification, coral reef restoration and mangrove replanting. Volunteers also use drones to map habitats and track climate change impacts. The base sits near the shoreline, surrounded by mangroves and dramatic limestone cliffs.

No diving certification or prior experience is needed. GVI Foundation Grants can cover up to 80 percent of programme costs (up to 13,000 USD), with applications open until March 31.

## Wildlife Rescue Centres

**Wildlife Friends Foundation Thailand (WFFT)** in Phetchaburi province runs one of the country's most respected rescue operations. The centre cares for more than 10 species of primates including gibbons, macaques and orangutans, along with bears, otters, civets, reptiles and birds. WFFT also operates Thailand's first dedicated wildlife hospital.

Volunteers help with feeding, cleaning enclosures and building behavioural enrichments. Physical contact with animals is discouraged — the focus is on ethical rehabilitation, not photo opportunities.

**Pod Volunteer** offers wildlife rescue placements with flexible start dates every Sunday year-round, for stays of one to twelve weeks.

**GoEco's Asian Wildlife Rescue Center** cares for around 800 animals. Volunteers assist with food preparation, enclosure maintenance and enrichment activities across the facility.

## Ethical Elephant Sanctuaries

**Phuket Elephant Sanctuary**, described as the first ethical elephant sanctuary in Phuket, accepts volunteers who want to help care for rescued elephants. Tasks include preparing food, maintaining habitats and observing elephant behaviour — all without riding or bathing the animals.

For those heading north, GVI runs elephant conservation internships in the village of Huay Pakoot near Chiang Mai, combining community engagement with hands-on wildlife research.

## How to Choose a Reputable Programme

Not all volunteer programmes are created equal. Before signing up, look for these signs of a responsible operation:

- **No direct animal contact for entertainment** — ethical centres prioritise rehabilitation over tourist interaction
- **Transparent finances** — reputable organisations explain where your fees go
- **Partnerships with local communities** — the best programmes involve and benefit local people
- **Minimum stay requirements** — programmes asking for at least one to two weeks tend to be more serious than single-day tourist experiences

## Getting Started

Most programmes accept applications online and require only basic fitness and a willingness to work outdoors. Costs vary from around 500 USD per week (including accommodation and meals) to higher-end research internships. Booking at least two to three months ahead is recommended for peak season placements.

Whether you spend a week tracking sea turtles in Trang or a month helping rescued gibbons in Phetchaburi, wildlife volunteering is one of the most rewarding ways to experience Thailand beyond the beaches and temples.
