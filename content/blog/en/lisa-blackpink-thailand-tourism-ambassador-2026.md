The Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) has officially appointed **Lisa Manobal**, the Thai-born global superstar from K-pop group BLACKPINK, as the **Amazing Thailand Ambassador** for 2026. The appointment was unveiled at a major event on January 28, 2026, attended by nearly 100 international media representatives, influencers, and opinion leaders.

## The "Lisa Effect" on Tourism

Thailand is banking big on what officials call the **"Lisa Effect"** — the ability of one of the world's most followed celebrities to convert fan interest into real travel demand. The TAT projects this collaboration could attract between **5 to 10 million additional foreign tourists**, generating revenue of **250 to 500 billion baht** (approximately $8 to $16 billion USD). The impact is already visible: the [Red Lotus Sea in Udon Thani reported record visitor numbers](/blog/red-lotus-sea-udon-thani-lisa-effect-record-visitors-2026/) directly attributed to Lisa's influence.

Lisa, born Lalisa Manobal in [Buriram province](/best-hotels/buriram/), has over 100 million followers across social media platforms. Her authentic Thai roots and global reach make her a natural fit for the campaign.

## The Campaign

The partnership goes beyond a standard celebrity endorsement. A dedicated **tourism advertising film** has been produced, designed to promote travel across Thailand throughout 2026. The campaign was developed around the concept of making Thailand **"felt, not just seen"** — encouraging deeper, more immersive travel experiences.

The TAT unveiled the campaign at a spectacular event at [**Wat Arun** (Temple of Dawn)](/blog/wat-arun-bangkok-temple-of-dawn-guide/) in [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/), one of the country's most iconic landmarks. The event was designed to connect Lisa's image with Thailand's cultural heritage.

## What Destinations Are Featured?

The campaign highlights both well-known and off-the-beaten-path destinations across Thailand, encouraging visitors to explore beyond Bangkok and the popular islands. Featured destinations include:

- **[Buriram](/best-hotels/buriram/)** — Lisa's hometown in Isan (northeastern Thailand)
- **[Chiang Mai](/city/chiang-mai/)** — cultural capital of the north
- **[Bangkok](/city/bangkok/)** — temple tours, street food, and nightlife
- **Southern islands** — from [Phuket](/city/phuket/) to lesser-known gems

The emphasis on secondary cities and regional destinations aligns with Thailand's broader **sustainable tourism strategy** for 2026, which aims to distribute tourism income more evenly across the country.

## Sustainable Tourism Push

The Lisa campaign is part of Thailand's wider **"The New Thailand"** vision, which focuses on quality over quantity. Alongside the celebrity partnership, the TAT is promoting initiatives like:

- **[Chiang Khan](/city/chiang-khan/)** in Loei province, [recognized at ITB Berlin 2026](/blog/the-new-thailand-itb-berlin-2026-sustainable-wellness-tourism/) for sustainable riverside tourism
- The [**Mekong Riverside** project across seven provinces](/blog/thailand-mekong-riverside-seven-provinces-sustainable-tourism-2026/)
- **Themed train journeys** such as the Royal Blossom and [Blue Jasmine routes](/blog/blue-jasmine-luxury-train-bangkok-chiang-mai-2026/)

These initiatives aim to attract travelers interested in cultural immersion and environmental responsibility rather than mass tourism.
