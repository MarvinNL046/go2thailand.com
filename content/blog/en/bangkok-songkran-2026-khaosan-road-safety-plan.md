Bangkok officials have agreed on a joint safety plan for this year's **Songkran celebrations on Khaosan Road**, one of the most popular water festival spots for international visitors. With Songkran 2026 running from **April 11 to 17**, multiple agencies are coordinating to ensure a safer festival experience.

## What's New This Year

The plan represents the most comprehensive safety approach for Khaosan Road's Songkran celebrations to date, addressing lessons learned from previous years when overcrowding and alcohol-related incidents marred the festivities.

### Security and Crowd Control

**CCTV cameras** will monitor the area 24 hours a day throughout the festival period. Screening points will be set up at entry zones, and emergency exits have been designated to help disperse crowds if density becomes too high.

Medical support teams, first-aid stations, and ambulances will be positioned across the area. Staff will be deployed to maintain safety and assist visitors.

### Traffic Management

Officials have agreed to set up a **dedicated service lane** for emergency vehicle access. Entry and exit routes will be clearly marked to reduce crowd build-up around the water festivities. Direction signs and information notices will be installed at regular intervals in both Thai and English.

## The "5 Por" Campaign

Authorities are promoting the **"5 Por" campaign** (five prohibitions), calling for a Songkran celebration free from:

1. **High-pressure water guns** that can cause injury
2. **Alcohol** in the festival zone
3. **Nudity** or inappropriate clothing
4. **Powder** (talcum throwing)
5. **Weapons** of any kind

## Road Safety: The "Seven Dangerous Days"

The Songkran period is traditionally known as the **"Seven Dangerous Days"** due to a sharp spike in road accidents across Thailand. The "Safe Songkran" initiative means **24/7 DUI checkpoints** and vehicle seizures for drunk drivers.

If you're visiting Bangkok for Songkran, public transport is strongly recommended. The BTS Skytrain and MRT are the safest and most efficient ways to reach Khaosan Road and other celebration areas.

## Tips for Visitors

- **Arrive early**: Khaosan Road gets extremely crowded after noon
- **Use a waterproof phone pouch**: essential for protecting your valuables
- **Wear quick-dry clothing and water shoes**: you will get soaked
- **Stay hydrated**: the April heat combined with water fights is exhausting
- **Keep copies of your passport** in your hotel safe — carry only a photocopy
- **Respect the culture**: Songkran is a religious holiday at its core, so be mindful of monks and temple ceremonies
