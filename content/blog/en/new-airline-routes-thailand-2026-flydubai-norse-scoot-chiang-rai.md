## Thailand's Aviation Boom

Thailand's Civil Aviation Authority has designated 2026 as the "peak recovery year" for the country's aviation sector. After years of reduced capacity following the pandemic, airlines are now racing to add routes and frequencies to Thai airports. The result is more choice and stronger competition on fares for travellers heading to [Thailand](/thailand-first-time-visitors-essential-guide-2026/).

The timing matters: [fare hikes from Thai carriers](/blog/thai-airways-fare-hike-10-15-percent-fuel-costs-2026/) and the broader [Middle East conflict's impact on airfare](/blog/middle-east-conflict-thailand-flights-airfare-2026/) have pushed many travellers to seek alternative carriers and routing options. New entrants help absorb that demand.

## Norse Atlantic Airways Opens Europe

Norwegian long-haul carrier Norse Atlantic Airways launched five new routes to Thailand for the 2025/2026 winter season. The airline operates affordable transatlantic-style services using modern Boeing 787 Dreamliners, offering European travellers a budget-friendly alternative to legacy carriers for reaching [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/).

The routes connect several European cities directly to Suvarnabhumi Airport, filling a gap left by airlines that never fully restored their pre-pandemic Thailand schedules. Norse Atlantic's competitive pricing has been welcomed by budget-conscious travellers who previously had to rely on connecting flights through Middle Eastern hubs. [Over 1,000 flights were cancelled during the Gulf crisis](/blog/thailand-aerothai-1000-flights-cancelled-gulf-crisis-2026/), underscoring why alternative routing through European carriers matters.

## Scoot Launches Chiang Rai Service

Singapore Airlines' low-cost subsidiary Scoot began operating five weekly flights between Singapore and [Chiang Rai](/city/chiang-rai/) in January 2026. This is a notable development because Chiang Rai's Mae Fah Luang Airport has historically received limited international service. It mirrors a parallel move by the parent group: [Singapore Airlines also launched nonstop flights to Chiang Mai](/blog/singapore-airlines-chiang-mai-nonstop-flights-2026/) in 2026, a sign of growing confidence in northern Thailand's tourism pull.

The new route makes it easier for travellers from Singapore and connecting destinations across Southeast Asia to reach northern Thailand without routing through Bangkok or Chiang Mai. [Chiang Rai](/city/chiang-rai/) is home to the famous [White Temple](/city/chiang-rai/attractions/white-temple/), the [Blue Temple](/city/chiang-rai/attractions/blue-temple/), and serves as a gateway to the [Golden Triangle](/city/chiang-rai/attractions/golden-triangle/) region.

## flydubai and Vietjet Add Capacity

Dubai-based flydubai is among the carriers expanding into Thailand during 2026, connecting the Gulf region with [Bangkok](/city/bangkok/). Meanwhile, Vietnamese low-cost carrier Vietjet continues to build out its Thai network, adding frequencies on routes between Vietnam and multiple Thai cities.

These additions are particularly important given the disruptions to some Middle Eastern flight corridors — including the [ongoing impact of the oil crisis on flight prices](/blog/thailand-oil-crisis-flight-prices-energy-march-2026/). Having more carriers serving the region increases resilience and gives passengers alternative routing options. [Thai carriers have also warned that a fuel surge could drive up ticket prices](/blog/thai-carriers-fuel-surge-tourism-boom-warning-2026/), making budget alternatives from flydubai and Norse Atlantic especially timely.

## What This Means for Your Trip

The growing number of airlines serving Thailand means more options when booking your flight. Here are some practical takeaways:

- **Northern Thailand access**: The Scoot service to [Chiang Rai](/city/chiang-rai/) removes the need to fly via Bangkok if you are coming from Singapore or connecting cities.
- **Budget long-haul from Europe**: Norse Atlantic offers a genuine low-cost option for European travellers. Pair it with [Songkran airline discount deals](/blog/songkran-2026-airlines-discount-flights/) for the best fares.
- **Gulf connectivity**: flydubai provides an alternative to the major Gulf carriers, often at lower price points.
- **Fare competition**: More airlines on Thai routes generally pushes prices down, especially during shoulder season. Check [Koh Samui's growing international connections](/blog/koh-samui-airport-international-hub-bangkok-airways-2026/) if you are heading to the islands.

Check route availability on your preferred booking platform, as schedules for newer services can change. Booking directly with the airline often provides the best flexibility for changes.
