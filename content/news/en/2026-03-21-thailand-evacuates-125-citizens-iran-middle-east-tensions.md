Thailand evacuated **125 of its citizens from Iran** in early March as escalating Middle East tensions forced the closure of regional airspace and raised concerns about traveler safety. The operation underscores the broader geopolitical disruptions that are [already affecting Thailand's tourism industry](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-tourism-7-million-visitors-despite-middle-east-tensions/).

## The Evacuation

The Thai government organized two evacuation groups:

- **Group 1**: 69 Thai nationals departed Iran on **March 7**
- **Group 2**: 56 Thai nationals departed on **March 10**

Unable to use direct flights due to closed airspace over the conflict zone, the evacuees traveled **overland through Turkey**, with the city of **Van** in eastern Turkey serving as the primary reception hub. Thailand established temporary consular operations there to process and assist the evacuees.

According to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/nieuws-uit-thailand/bangkok-haalt-125-landgenoten-uit-iran-via-turkije/), the Thai government set up a crisis coordination center to manage the operation, suggesting officials expect the disruption to persist.

## Why This Matters for Travelers

The Middle East conflict is creating ripple effects across global travel:

**Closed airspace**: Multiple countries in the region have restricted or completely closed their airspace to commercial flights. This forces airlines to reroute, adding flight time and fuel costs to routes between Europe and Asia.

**Flight price increases**: As we reported earlier, airline ticket prices on [Thailand-Europe routes have jumped up to 15%](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-tourism-7-million-visitors-despite-middle-east-tensions/) as carriers absorb higher fuel costs and longer routing.

**Fuel supply chain**: The same tensions are driving [Thailand's domestic fuel crisis](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-fuel-crisis-update-diesel-85-percent-surge/), with oil prices climbing as Strait of Hormuz shipping routes face uncertainty.

## Thailand Itself Remains Safe

It's important to distinguish between the regional geopolitical situation and conditions within Thailand:

- **All major tourist destinations** (Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, Koh Samui, Krabi) are completely unaffected
- **Airports operate normally** — Suvarnabhumi, Don Mueang, and regional airports are at full capacity
- **No travel advisories** have been issued for Thailand itself by any Western government
- **The only impact** is indirect: higher flight costs from some origin countries and fuel price increases domestically

## Practical Advice

If you're flying to Thailand from Europe in the coming weeks:

- **Book flights early** — prices are volatile and trending upward
- **Check your routing** — some flights may take longer if rerouted to avoid Middle Eastern airspace
- **Gulf hub connections** (Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi) remain operational but monitor for changes
- **Consider Asian-routed alternatives** — flights via Singapore, Hong Kong, or Seoul avoid Middle Eastern airspace entirely
- **Travel insurance** is more important than ever — ensure your policy covers trip disruption due to geopolitical events

Thailand's quick evacuation response demonstrates the government's capacity to protect its citizens abroad — a reassuring signal for the millions of international visitors who travel to the country each year.
