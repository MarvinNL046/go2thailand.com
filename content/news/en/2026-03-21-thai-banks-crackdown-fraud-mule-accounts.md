Thai banks are tightening digital transfer controls as part of a nationwide crackdown on "mule accounts" — intermediary bank accounts used by criminals to rapidly move stolen funds before victims or institutions can intervene.

## New Tiered Transfer Limits

The Bank of Thailand has introduced a **risk-based classification system** for mobile banking customers. Everyone is placed into one of three tiers:

| Tier | Daily Mobile Transfer Limit | Profile |
|------|---------------------------|---------|
| **S (Small)** | Under 50,000 THB | New accounts, limited banking history |
| **M (Medium)** | 50,000–200,000 THB | Established customers with regular activity |
| **L (Large)** | Over 200,000 THB | Long-term customers with verified income |

Your tier is determined by your banking behavior and how much customer due diligence (KYC) you've completed. This means new account holders — including foreigners who recently opened Thai bank accounts — will likely start at the S tier.

## Kasikornbank Closes Multi-App Loophole

Kasikornbank (KBank) is unifying daily transfer limits across all three of its digital platforms: **K PLUS**, **Make by KBank**, and **Line BK**. Previously, users could potentially circumvent individual app limits by spreading transfers across multiple platforms. The unified limit closes this gap.

## Bangkok Bank Reverses Minimum Balance Rule

In a notable reversal, **Bangkok Bank** shelved its plan to require a **2,000 baht minimum balance** for digital savings accounts (e-Savings and Bualuang Extra Digital Savings). The rule, announced for April 9, 2026, would have blocked any transaction that reduced an account balance below the threshold.

Following significant backlash on social media — with customers pointing out the inconvenience for those who regularly empty accounts — Bangkok Bank **indefinitely postponed** the requirement, acknowledging customer concerns.

## Is the Crackdown Working?

According to industry data cited by [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/nieuws-uit-thailand/thaise-banken-voeren-druk-op-in-strijd-tegen-muilezelaccounts/), total banking fraud losses have declined from approximately **3 billion baht** to around **1.8 billion baht** in 2026 — a meaningful reduction, though the problem remains significant.

## What This Means for Expats and Digital Nomads

If you have a Thai bank account or are planning to open one:

- **Transfer limits may be lower than expected** for new accounts. If you need to make large transfers (e.g., paying rent), ensure your account has been upgraded to M or L tier by completing full KYC verification
- **Bangkok Bank's minimum balance rule is on hold** — no action needed for now, but keep an eye on announcements
- **Multiple banking apps** from the same bank now share a single daily limit
- **ATM withdrawals** for foreign cards are unaffected by these changes — the standard 220 THB fee per withdrawal remains
- **Wise and international transfers** into Thai accounts continue to work normally

These measures are anti-fraud protections, not restrictions on legitimate banking. If you use your account for normal everyday transactions, you're unlikely to be affected. The changes primarily target accounts showing suspicious patterns — rapid deposits followed by immediate withdrawal across multiple channels.
