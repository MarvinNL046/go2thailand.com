Thailand has been planning a **300-baht (~$8 USD) entry fee** for all foreign visitors — but the launch date keeps shifting. Here's everything travelers need to know about the so-called "Kha Yeap Pan Din" (literally: "stepping on Thai soil" fee) as of March 2026.

## What Is the Fee?

The Thai government approved a 300-baht levy on all international tourists arriving by air, land, or sea. The fee breaks down as follows:

- **70 baht**: covers immediate medical and accident insurance from the moment you enter Thailand
- **230 baht**: funds improvements to tourism infrastructure, public amenities, and safety systems across the country

The insurance component is notable — it means every tourist would automatically receive basic medical coverage upon arrival, regardless of whether they carry their own travel insurance.

## When Does It Start?

This is where it gets complicated. The fee has been announced and delayed multiple times:

- **Original plan**: The fee was first proposed several years ago
- **February 2026**: [Chiang Rai Times](https://www.chiangraitimes.com/national/thailand-entry-fee-2026/) reported the fee would launch in February 2026
- **Delayed again**: The Tourism Ministry [pushed the rollout](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/tourism/40052562) to mid-2026, citing concerns about weak tourism demand recovery
- **Current status** (March 2026): The fee has **not yet been implemented**. No firm start date has been announced

The repeated delays suggest the Thai government is cautious about adding any barrier — even a small one — while tourism numbers are still recovering toward pre-pandemic levels.

## How Will It Be Collected?

According to reports from [Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/thailands-new-entry-fee-in-2026-a-bold-step-to-boost-tourism-infrastructure-offer-medical-coverage-and-improve-safety-for-international-travelers/), the fee will be collected through:

- **Airlines**: added to ticket purchases or collected during check-in
- **Border checkpoints**: paid at immigration for land and sea arrivals

The exact collection mechanism hasn't been finalized, which is one reason for the ongoing delays.

## What It Means for Travelers

**Right now**: No fee is being charged. You can enter Thailand without paying anything beyond your normal visa requirements (most nationalities get 60 days visa-free).

**When it launches**: Expect to pay 300 baht (~$8 USD) once per entry. This is a one-time fee per visit, not daily. For context, neighboring countries charge similar fees — Cambodia charges $30 for a visa on arrival, and Bali (Indonesia) charges a tourist levy.

**The upside**: The built-in medical insurance (70 baht portion) means every tourist gets basic accident and emergency coverage automatically — a genuinely useful benefit, especially for budget travelers who might otherwise skip insurance.

## Should You Worry?

No. At 300 baht (~$8 USD), the fee is minimal compared to total trip costs. If you're already budgeting for flights, accommodation, and activities, this won't meaningfully affect your plans. The automatic medical insurance component actually adds value.

The main uncertainty is timing. If you're planning a trip to Thailand in 2026, check the latest updates before departure — the fee could launch with relatively short notice once the collection system is ready.
