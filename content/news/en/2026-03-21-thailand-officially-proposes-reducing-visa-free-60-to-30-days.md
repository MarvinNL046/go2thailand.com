**Update to our [earlier reporting](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-60-day-visa-free-may-be-reduced-to-30/):** Thailand's Foreign Minister **Sihasak Phuangketkeow** has now officially put forward a proposal to reduce the visa-free stay from **60 days back to 30 days** for all eligible nationalities.

The proposal is "on the table" for government consideration but **has not yet been enacted**. The 60-day visa-free stay remains in effect as of today.

## What the Foreign Minister Said

According to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/toerisme/thailand-wil-visumvrij-verblijf-inkorten-tot-30-dagen/), the government's reasons for the proposed reduction center on **misuse of the generous entry policy**:

- **Illegal work**: Foreigners using visa-free stays to conduct business activities, run unlicensed operations, or take jobs in sectors restricted to Thai workers
- **Property fraud**: Schemes involving unlawful property ownership by foreigners
- **Online fraud operations**: Security concerns about criminal networks exploiting easy entry
- **General security**: Difficulty tracking long-stay visitors who may overstay or engage in illegal activities

## What Would Change

If the proposal is adopted:

| | Current (60 days) | Proposed (30 days) |
|--|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Initial stay** | 60 days visa-free | 30 days visa-free |
| **Extension** | +30 days (1,900 THB) | +30 days (1,900 THB) |
| **Maximum** | 90 days total | 60 days total |

The **30-day extension option** would remain available at immigration offices for 1,900 THB — meaning travelers who need more than 30 days can still get up to 60 days total, but with the extra step of visiting immigration.

## Who Would Be Most Affected

**Short-stay tourists (1-3 weeks):** Not affected at all. Most vacationers don't stay beyond 30 days.

**Long-stay travelers most impacted:**
- **"Winterers"** — Europeans and retirees who spend 2-3 months in Thailand during cold season back home
- **Digital nomads** without a [DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/digital-nomad-visa/) — those relying on visa-free entry for extended stays
- **Travelers combining Thailand with regional trips** — the 60-day window gave flexibility for multi-country itineraries with Thailand as a base

## What You Should Do

**If you're traveling now:** Nothing changes yet. The 60-day visa-free stay is still active.

**If you're planning a longer trip:**

1. **Apply for a Tourist Visa** (60 days, ~2,000 THB) before departure — this guarantees your stay length regardless of policy changes
2. **Consider the [DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/blog/digital-nomad-thailand-2026-dtv-visa-costs-cities/)** if you're a remote worker — 180 days per entry, valid for 5 years
3. **Don't build itineraries** that depend on 60 visa-free days — the change could come with relatively short notice
4. **Complete the [Thailand Digital Arrival Card](https://tdac.immigration.go.th/arrival-card/#/home)** regardless — it's required for all entries

## Timeline Uncertainty

No implementation date has been announced. Based on how Thai policy changes typically unfold:

- The proposal needs cabinet approval
- An implementation timeline would follow (likely 30-90 days notice)
- Changes could take effect **as early as Q3 2026** if the government moves quickly, or be delayed indefinitely if tourism numbers drop further
