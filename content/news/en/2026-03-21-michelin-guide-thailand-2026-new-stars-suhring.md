The **Michelin Guide Thailand 2026** has been unveiled, with **10 new star additions** and a landmark promotion: Bangkok's **Sühring** becomes only the second restaurant in the country to earn **three Michelin stars**.

## The Headlines

**468 dining venues** made the 2026 guide — a comprehensive recognition of Thailand's food scene that spans from elite fine dining to street-side noodle stalls.

### Star Breakdown

| Category | Count | Change |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Three Stars | 2 | +1 (Sühring promoted) |
| Two Stars | 8 | +2 promoted |
| One Star | 33 | +3 newcomers, +4 promoted |
| Bib Gourmand | 137 | +13 newcomers |
| Michelin Selected | 288 | +50 newcomers |

## Sühring: Bangkok's New Three-Star

The biggest news is **Sühring**, the restaurant founded by German twin chefs **Thomas and Mathias Sühring**, earning its promotion from two to three stars. Located in a converted residential house on Yen Akat Road, Sühring serves a modern German tasting menu inspired by family recipes, childhood memories, and seasonal ingredients prepared using traditional techniques like fermenting, pickling, and curing.

The three-star designation places Sühring alongside the legendary **Gaggan Anand** (which held three stars in previous editions) at the absolute pinnacle of Thai dining, according to [Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/life/food/40061551).

## Notable New One-Star Entries

Several restaurants earned their first Michelin star in 2026:

- **Cannubi by Umberto Bombana** — Italian fine dining from the Hong Kong-based chef
- **etcha** — Innovative Thai cuisine
- **Nusara** — Modern Thai with traditional roots
- **Sushi Saito** — Tokyo-standard omakase in Bangkok
- **Juksunchae** — Korean fine dining

## Street Food Still Matters

The Michelin Guide's **Bib Gourmand** category — honoring restaurants that offer excellent food at moderate prices — added **13 newcomers** in 2026, bringing the total to 137. The latest list highlights **16 distinct cuisine types**, reflecting Thailand's extraordinary culinary diversity.

**Jay Fai** remains Bangkok's most iconic Michelin-recognized street food vendor. Her [golden crab omelette](https://go2-thailand.com/food/) and stir-fried prawns in yellow curry continue to draw queues that stretch down the block. Despite Michelin recognition and prices that are high by street food standards (1,000+ THB per dish), the experience remains quintessentially Bangkok.

## What This Means for Food Travelers

Thailand's Michelin recognition continues to strengthen the country's position as one of the world's great food destinations. For travelers:

**Fine dining is world-class**: With 2 three-star and 8 two-star restaurants, [Bangkok](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/) now competes with Tokyo, Paris, and New York for top-tier dining. Reservations at starred restaurants should be made weeks or months in advance.

**Bib Gourmand is where the value is**: The 137 Bib Gourmand restaurants offer outstanding food at prices that would be impossible in Western cities. Many of these are small, family-run operations serving specialized Thai dishes — exactly the kind of authentic eating experience that makes Thailand special.

**Street food remains king**: Despite the fine dining growth, Thailand's real food story is still on the street. A [pad thai](https://go2-thailand.com/food/pad-thai/) from a dedicated vendor costs 40-60 baht and can be just as memorable as a starred meal — a point the Michelin Guide increasingly acknowledges through its street food selections.
