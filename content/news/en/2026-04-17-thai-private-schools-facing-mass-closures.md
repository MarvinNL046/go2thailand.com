The announcement of mass closures points to broader pressures on the private education sector. While the Bangkok Post report does not specify causes, widely known demographic trends in Thailand provide context. The country has one of the world's lowest fertility rates, leading to a shrinking school-age population over the past decade. This decline in potential enrollment is a public challenge acknowledged in national discussions on education and economics. Additionally, public schools have seen improvements in quality and affordability, potentially drawing students away from pricier private options. Post-pandemic shifts, such as remote learning adaptations and economic uncertainties, may have further strained finances for these institutions, though this is speculation based on general trends rather than report specifics.

Private schools typically operate on tuition fees, making them vulnerable to enrollment drops. Without government subsidies like those for public schools, even small declines can lead to insolvency.
