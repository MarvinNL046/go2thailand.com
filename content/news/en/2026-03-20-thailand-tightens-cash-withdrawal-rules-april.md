Thailand is introducing stricter rules for large cash withdrawals starting **April 1, 2026**, as the Bank of Thailand moves to combat money laundering and fraud. While the changes primarily target high-value transactions, travelers and expats should be aware of the new framework.

## What's Changing?

Under the new regulations, **cash withdrawals of 5 million baht or more in a single day** will be classified as high-risk transactions. Banks and financial institutions will be required to conduct enhanced customer due diligence on these withdrawals, including potentially requesting documentation about the source of funds, the purpose of the withdrawal, and why the customer prefers cash over electronic transfer.

Banks can refuse transactions if the documentation provided is insufficient, and suspicious cases can be reported directly to anti-money laundering authorities.

## New Foreign Exchange Limits

The rules also set clear daily limits for foreign exchange transactions:

- **General daily limit:** 800,000 baht per person
- **Border areas:** 200,000 baht per person daily

These limits apply to both Thai nationals and foreigners exchanging currency at banks, exchange booths, and other licensed operators.

## Why Is This Happening?

The [Bank of Thailand](https://www.bot.or.th/) flagged unusually large cash withdrawals totaling over 250 million baht in recent reports, with some requests specifically asking for 500-baht notes or smaller denominations — a pattern often associated with money laundering or untraceable illegal financial flows.

The new measures bring Thailand's cash transaction monitoring closer in line with international anti-money laundering standards.

## What It Means for Travelers

For most tourists, these changes won't affect daily life. Standard ATM withdrawals (typically 10,000–30,000 baht per transaction) remain unchanged, as do the existing 220 baht foreign ATM fees.

However, travelers should note:

- **Foreign exchange limits** of 800,000 baht per day could affect those exchanging large amounts of currency at once
- **Enhanced identity verification** is now required at all financial touchpoints — expect to show your passport when exchanging money or making large transactions
- **Electronic payments** continue to be encouraged over cash, with banks required to verify identity through PIN codes, one-time codes, app verification, or biometric authentication for electronic channels

## Practical Tips

- Keep your passport accessible when visiting banks or exchange booths
- Use ATMs for routine cash needs — no changes to standard withdrawal limits
- Consider electronic payment methods (credit cards, mobile banking) for larger purchases
- If exchanging large amounts, split transactions across multiple days if needed to stay within limits

The regulations take effect **April 1, 2026** and apply to all commercial banks, financial institutions, and licensed currency exchange operators in Thailand.
