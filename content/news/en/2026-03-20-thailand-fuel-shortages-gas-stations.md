Gas stations across Thailand are struggling with fuel shortages, with over 70% of inspected stations either completely or nearly out of specific fuels. The disruption is hitting motorists and travelers hard — but authorities say the underlying problem isn't a lack of supply.

## What's Happening?

During inspections of **2,649 gas stations** across the country, Thailand's Ministry of Energy found that **1,912 stations** were entirely or nearly out of specific fuel types. An additional **241 stations** had temporarily closed altogether.

The shortages are nationwide, though stations in areas like northern Ubon Ratchathani and central Pathum Thani have been particularly affected. Diesel appears to be the most severely impacted fuel.

## Why There's No Fuel — Despite Full Reserves

According to adjunct director-general Wutthitat Tantivess, the country's national fuel reserves are sufficient. The problem lies in two factors:

1. **Slow distribution**: Supply chains can't keep up with sudden demand spikes, creating bottlenecks between refineries and retail stations
2. **Panic buying**: Fears about rising oil prices — driven by Middle East tensions — have prompted motorists to fill up more frequently than usual, overwhelming the system

The result is a distribution crisis, not a supply crisis. Fuel exists at the refinery level but isn't reaching stations fast enough.

## Government Response

Thai authorities have implemented several emergency measures:

- **Extended Bangkok tank truck delivery hours to 24/7** to speed up distribution
- **Coordinating** between refineries and station operators on pricing and allocation
- **Gradually raising diesel prices** from 30 to 33 baht per liter to reduce subsidy pressure and discourage hoarding

## Impact on Travelers

For tourists renting cars or motorcycles, the shortages mean:

- **Long queues** at gas stations, especially in popular tourist areas
- **Purchase limits** of 500–1,000 baht per vehicle at some stations
- **Station closures**: check ahead before counting on a particular station
- **Higher prices**: diesel has risen from 30 to 33 baht per liter

### Practical Tips

- **Keep your tank above half** — don't wait until near-empty to refuel
- **Use Google Maps** to check which stations are open and avoid closed ones
- **Consider public transport**: buses, trains, and domestic flights are unaffected by retail fuel shortages
- **If renting a scooter**, fill up whenever you pass an open station rather than waiting
- **Island travelers**: ferry services may be affected if shortages persist — check schedules before booking

The situation is evolving, and authorities expect distribution to normalize as panic buying subsides and the extended delivery schedules take effect.
