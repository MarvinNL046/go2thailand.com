# Thailand Cuts Visa-Free Stay to 30 Days for UK and 92 Other Nations

Citizens of the **United Kingdom** and nationals from **92 other countries** will soon need to apply for a visa if they wish to stay in Thailand for longer than 30 days, according to [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c78q3qjqnn5o). The Thai government approved the change on Tuesday, ending a 60-day visa exemption that has been in place since July 2024.

The policy shift marks a significant reversal of Thailand's post-pandemic tourism strategy. Prime Minister **Anutin Charnvirakul** said the government found the existing policy needed revising "to be more suitable for the current situation, both in terms of the economy and national security."

## Background: The 60-Day Exemption Era

Since July 2024, tourists from 93 countries have been able to visit Thailand without a visa for up to 60 days as part of a government initiative to revive the tourism sector following the Covid-19 pandemic. The exemption applied to nationals from major tourism source markets including **Australia, China, France, Germany, India, Italy, Spain, and the United States**.

Thailand is one of Asia's most popular holiday destinations, with tourism playing a crucial role in the nation's economy. The country welcomed nearly 40 million visitors in 2019 before pandemic-related disruptions. Recovery has been gradual but steady—nearly 12 million people have already visited Thailand so far this year, according to official figures.

## Why the Change?

The Thai government cited two main reasons for ending the extended exemption: national security concerns and confusion created by overlapping visa schemes.

A series of high-profile arrests of foreign nationals has prompted the security review. Recent cases have included charges linked to drugs and sex trafficking. In April, Thai police raided an unlicensed international school in Bangkok and arrested 10 foreigners employed there without work permits. Additionally, UK citizens have been accused of drug smuggling offences in recent months.

The foreign ministry also stated it aimed to reduce instances of multiple visa exemptions applying to the same country, which it said "confuse foreigners."

## What Changes for Travelers

Under the new system, most nationals from the affected 93 countries will be required to obtain a visa for stays exceeding 30 days. However, the Thai foreign ministry indicated that exemption periods may vary by country based on reciprocal bilateral agreements. Some nationalities may receive shorter or longer exemption periods than the standard 30 days.

The new visa framework will come into effect 15 days after it appears in the Royal Gazette, Thailand's official publication for legal and regulatory notifications. This means travelers should monitor official channels for the exact implementation date.

For those planning extended stays in Thailand, the change means additional administrative steps and potential costs associated with visa applications. Visitors planning trips of 30 days or fewer will not be immediately affected by this policy change.

## What's Next

The Thai government has not yet published detailed country-by-country exemption schedules, though the foreign ministry is expected to clarify specific arrangements following the Royal Gazette announcement. Travelers are advised to check official Thai immigration resources and their respective embassy websites for updated guidance on their nationality's specific exemption period.

The policy reversal reflects Thailand's attempt to balance tourism recovery with domestic security priorities—a challenge facing many popular tourist destinations in Southeast Asia.
