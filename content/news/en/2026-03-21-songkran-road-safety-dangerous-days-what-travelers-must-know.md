Every Songkran, Thailand confronts a grim reality: the festival period known as the **"Seven Dangerous Days"** (April 11-17) consistently produces the country's deadliest stretch of road accidents. If you're traveling in Thailand during Songkran 2026, road safety awareness could literally save your life.

## The Numbers

During last year's Seven Dangerous Days, Thailand recorded:

- **287 road fatalities**
- **2,060 injuries**
- **2,044 traffic accidents**

[Chiang Rai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-rai/) saw the highest accident count with 82 incidents and 17 deaths. Seven of Thailand's 77 provinces managed zero fatalities — proving that effective enforcement makes a difference.

These numbers come from the [Bangkok Post](https://www.bangkokpost.com/thailand/general/2983430/songkran-road-safety-requires-tireless-effort), which notes that road safety during Songkran "requires tireless effort" from authorities and citizens alike.

## Why Songkran Is So Dangerous

Three factors converge during Songkran to create Thailand's deadliest traffic period:

**1. Mass migration**
Millions of Thais travel from Bangkok and other cities to their home provinces for the New Year holiday. Highways that normally handle moderate traffic become overwhelmed with cars, buses, and motorcycles — many driven by tired drivers covering long distances.

**2. Alcohol**
Songkran is a celebration, and alcohol flows freely. Despite anti-drunk driving campaigns, DUI remains the leading cause of Songkran road deaths. The festival's water-throwing tradition adds to the chaos — wet roads, obscured vision, and distracted drivers compound the risk.

**3. Motorcycles**
Motorcycles account for a disproportionate share of Songkran casualties. Many riders are young, unlicensed, or riding without helmets — and water splashing at motorcyclists on main roads creates immediate danger.

## What Authorities Are Doing in 2026

The Thai government is deploying its most comprehensive Songkran safety campaign yet:

- **24/7 DUI checkpoints** across major highways and city streets
- **Vehicle seizure** for drunk drivers — your car or motorcycle can be impounded on the spot
- **Ban on water-throwing from moving vehicles** in many city centers, including Bangkok
- **The "5 Por" campaign** in [Bangkok's Khao San Road](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-bangkok-songkran-2026-khao-san-road-safety-plan/) area: no high-pressure guns, alcohol in festival zones, nudity, powder, or weapons
- **Royal Thai Police** deployed in force during the April 11-17 period

## How Travelers Can Stay Safe

### If You're Driving

- **Avoid driving on April 12-14** if possible — these are the peak accident days
- **Never ride a motorbike during Songkran** water fights — wet roads + water in your face = crashes
- **Don't accept rides from visibly intoxicated drivers** (including tuk-tuk and songthaew operators)
- **Use Grab instead of driving yourself** — let someone else navigate the chaos
- **If driving long-distance**, leave very early morning (before 6 AM) or wait until after dark when water fights stop

### If You're Walking or Celebrating

- **Wear shoes with grip** — streets become incredibly slippery when wet
- **Protect your phone and valuables** in waterproof bags
- **Don't throw water at motorcyclists** on roads — this genuinely causes accidents
- **Stay hydrated** — April is Thailand's hottest month (35-40°C)
- **Know the emergency number**: **1669** (medical) and **1155** (Tourist Police)

### The Safest Approach

The safest way to enjoy Songkran is to pick a fixed celebration area — [Khao San Road](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-bangkok-songkran-2026-khao-san-road-safety-plan/), Silom Road in Bangkok, or the moat area in [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) — and stay there for the day. Walk in, celebrate, walk out. Avoid being on the road during peak water-fight hours (10 AM - 6 PM).

Thailand's Songkran is an incredible cultural experience — one of the world's great festivals. But awareness of the road safety risks is part of being a responsible traveler. Stay off motorcycles, avoid driving during peak hours, and use ride-hailing apps when you need to move around.
