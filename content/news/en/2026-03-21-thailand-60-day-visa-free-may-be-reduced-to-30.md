Thailand's generous **60-day visa-free entry** — one of the most traveler-friendly policies in Southeast Asia — may not last. Thai authorities are actively reviewing the policy, with some officials pushing to revert to the previous **30-day limit**.

As of March 2026, the 60-day visa-free stay **remains in effect**. But travelers planning longer trips should be aware that changes could come with relatively little notice.

## Why the Review?

The 60-day visa exemption was extended in 2024 as part of Thailand's aggressive push to rebuild tourism after COVID-19. It applied to citizens of 93 countries, including the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, India, and most EU nations.

However, officials have raised several concerns, according to [Thai Examiner](https://www.thaiexaminer.com/thai-news-foreigners/2026/02/13/visa-free-entry-period-to-go-from-60-to-30-days-agreement-in-principle-as-foreign-tourism-numbers-fall/):

- **Illegal work**: Some visitors are using long visa-free stays to work without permits, running unlicensed businesses or taking jobs from Thai workers — particularly in tourism hubs like Phuket and Pattaya
- **Security concerns**: The extended stay has made it harder to track long-term visitors who may overstay or engage in illegal activities
- **Falling numbers**: International tourism arrivals are down approximately 4.4% in early 2026 compared to 2025, raising questions about whether the generous visa policy is actually working

According to [VisasNews](https://visasnews.com/en/thailand-authorities-reassessing-60-day-visa-exemption/), Thai authorities have reached an "agreement in principle" to reassess the policy, though no official date for any change has been announced.

## What Hasn't Changed (Yet)

As of today, these rules remain in effect:

- **60-day visa-free entry** for citizens of 93 countries arriving by air or land
- **30-day extension** available at immigration offices for 1,900 THB
- **Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)** must be completed before arrival at [tdac.immigration.go.th](https://tdac.immigration.go.th/arrival-card/#/home)
- **[DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/digital-nomad-visa/)** (180 days) remains available for digital nomads

## New 6-Month Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa

One positive development: Thailand has introduced a **new 6-month multiple-entry tourist visa**, allowing travelers to enter the country multiple times with up to 60 days per visit. This is ideal for travelers making multiple trips to Thailand within a year, according to [Chiang Rai Times](https://www.chiangraitimes.com/destinations/thailand-60-day-visa-rules/).

## What Should Travelers Do?

**If you're planning a trip now:**
- The 60-day visa-free entry is still active — book your trip normally
- If your trip depends on having 60 days, consider applying for a Tourist Visa (60 days guaranteed) rather than relying on the visa exemption
- Check [Thailand visa requirements](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/) for your nationality before booking

**If you're planning for later in 2026:**
- Monitor official announcements — changes could come with 30-60 days notice
- The [300-baht tourist entry fee](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-thailand-300-baht-tourist-entry-fee-update/) is also still pending, adding another variable to entry requirements
- Consider the DTV visa if you need guaranteed long-term access
