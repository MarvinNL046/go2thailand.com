With Songkran 2026 less than a month away (April 13–15), Bangkok officials have announced a comprehensive safety and traffic management plan for **Khao San Road** — the epicenter of Bangkok's water fight celebrations and one of the most popular Songkran destinations for international visitors.

## What's Being Planned

Multiple Bangkok agencies are coordinating to manage what is expected to be another record-breaking Songkran at Khao San Road. Last year's celebrations drew [over 650,000 visitors](https://www.nationthailand.com/thailand/bangkok/40048834) to the area across three days.

Key measures include:

- **Designated entry and exit points** to prevent dangerous crowd buildup
- **A dedicated emergency service lane** allowing ambulances and staff to access the area quickly
- **24-hour CCTV monitoring** of the entire festival zone
- **Screening checkpoints** at entry zones
- **First-aid stations** with ambulances on standby for emergencies
- **Directional signage** in multiple languages for tourists and residents

## The "5 Por" Campaign

Bangkok is promoting the **"5 Por" campaign** (ห้าป) for a safer celebration. The campaign calls for Songkran festivities free from:

1. **High-pressure water guns** (ปืนฉีดน้ำแรงดันสูง) — regular water guns are fine
2. **Alcohol** in public celebration areas
3. **Nudity** — keep it appropriate
4. **Powder** — the traditional chalk powder paste (din sor pong) is being discouraged
5. **Weapons** — zero tolerance

While enforcement details haven't been specified, the campaign signals authorities' intent to keep celebrations family-friendly and safe.

## What Travelers Should Know

If you're planning to celebrate Songkran at Khao San Road, here's what to expect:

### Getting There
- **BTS to Pha Fa Lilat** (closest station) or **taxi/Grab to Phra Athit Road** — then walk
- Expect road closures around Khao San Road during festival hours
- Plan your exit route before entering — crowds can make navigation difficult

### What to Bring
- A waterproof phone case or bag (essential)
- Lightweight, quick-dry clothing
- Waterproof sandals with good grip (streets get slippery)
- Cash in a waterproof pouch — many street vendors are cash-only
- Sunscreen and a hat for daytime celebrations

### What to Avoid
- Don't bring valuables you can't afford to get wet
- Avoid riding motorbikes during peak water fight hours
- Don't splash monks, elderly people, or anyone clearly not participating
- Be cautious with street food and drinks from unlicensed vendors

### Safety Tips
- Stay hydrated — it's the hottest month of the year (35°C+)
- Watch your belongings — pickpockets operate in dense crowds
- If you feel overwhelmed by crowd density, move toward the designated exit points
- Keep the Tourist Police number saved: **1155** (toll-free, 24/7)

## Alternatives to Khao San Road

If Khao San Road's intensity isn't your style, Bangkok offers other Songkran spots:

- **Silom Road**: equally massive, more organized, popular with Thai young professionals
- **Siam Square**: central location, family-friendly atmosphere
- **RCA (Royal City Avenue)**: younger crowd, club-oriented celebrations

Outside Bangkok, [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) is famous for its moat-centered water battles, while [Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/phuket/) offers a beach-town celebration vibe.
