If you're planning a Thailand trip in 2026 and hoping to cross into Cambodia overland — for Angkor Wat, a visa run, or a Southeast Asia circuit — you need to know: **all land border crossings between Thailand and Cambodia remain closed** as of March 2026.

## What Happened

The Thailand-Cambodia border crisis escalated in mid-2025 over longstanding territorial disputes, including the contested area around the [Preah Vihear temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Cambodian%E2%80%93Thai_border_crisis). Military tensions led to the closure of all official land border checkpoints in late June 2025.

A ceasefire was announced on **December 27, 2025**, calling for an immediate halt to military operations and mutual commitments to clearing land mines along the border. However, the ceasefire remains fragile, with sporadic incidents and general mistrust continuing into 2026.

## Which Border Crossings Are Closed?

**All official land crossings** between Thailand and Cambodia are closed, including the routes most commonly used by tourists:

- **Aranyaprathet / Poipet** — the most popular crossing for Bangkok to Siem Reap (Angkor Wat)
- **Hat Lek / Koh Kong** — the route from Trat province to Cambodia's coast
- **Chong Chom / O Smach** — the Surin province crossing
- **All other land checkpoints** along the border

## Is Thailand Safe?

**Yes.** The conflict is confined to the border zone. The [U.S. Embassy](https://th.usembassy.gov/security-alert-continued-armed-conflict-along-the-thailand-cambodia-border-2/) advises avoiding travel within 50 kilometers of the Thailand-Cambodia border, but all major tourist destinations are completely unaffected:

- **Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, Koh Samui, Krabi** — normal operations
- **Airports, trains, and attractions** — functioning as usual
- **No incidents** involving tourists in any major destination

The conflict's impact on daily life in Thailand is essentially zero outside the immediate border area.

## What About Visa Runs?

The border closure has eliminated the popular **Thailand-Cambodia visa run** (crossing to Poipet and returning for a new entry stamp). If you need to leave Thailand to reset your visa:

**Alternative visa run destinations:**
- **Laos** (Vientiane via Nong Khai or Chiang Khong) — land border open and functioning
- **Malaysia** (Padang Besar or Hat Yai to Penang) — fully operational
- **Myanmar** — some crossings open, check current status
- **By air** — fly to any neighboring country and return

Note: as of November 2025, [DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/digital-nomad-visa/) holders are limited to **2 visa runs per calendar year**.

## How to Combine Thailand and Cambodia in 2026

The only realistic option is to **fly**. Direct flights operate between:

- **Bangkok (BKK/DMK) to Phnom Penh** — multiple daily flights, ~1 hour
- **Bangkok to Siem Reap** — several daily flights, ~1 hour
- **Prices**: typically 2,000–5,000 THB one-way depending on advance booking

This adds cost compared to the old overland routes (which were 300–500 THB by bus), but it's the only option until land borders reopen.

## When Will Borders Reopen?

No official timeline has been announced. The ceasefire holds but negotiations are ongoing. Thailand's new government under [PM Anutin](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-anutin-reelected-thailand-prime-minister/) has signaled interest in diplomatic resolution, but the situation remains fluid.

**Practical advice:** Don't build your itinerary around overland Thailand-Cambodia travel in 2026. Plan to fly if you want to visit both countries, or treat them as separate trips.
