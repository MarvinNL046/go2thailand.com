Thailand's [ongoing fuel distribution crisis](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-thailand-fuel-shortages-gas-stations/) has reached Bangkok's main international airport. The number of taxis available at **Suvarnabhumi Airport** has **dropped from over 5,000 to approximately 2,500** — nearly halved — as drivers struggle to find fuel.

This is the seventh article in our [fuel crisis coverage](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-fuel-crisis-update-diesel-85-percent-surge/).

## What's Happening

Suvarnabhumi's taxi association normally has **5,000-6,000 registered taxis** serving arriving passengers. According to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/vliegtickets/brandstoftekort-halveert-taxiaanbod-op-luchthaven-suvarnabhumi/), that number has dropped to roughly **2,500 active vehicles** — a direct result of drivers being unable to reliably find LPG or NGV fuel at gas stations.

The problem hits hardest for:

- **Larger vehicles** (SUVs and vans) used by families and groups with luggage — these consume more fuel and are less economical when stations are unreliable
- **Longer routes** — drivers avoid taking passengers to distant destinations because they can't guarantee finding fuel for the return trip
- **Peak arrival times** — when multiple international flights land simultaneously, the reduced fleet creates longer queues

## Why This Matters for Arriving Travelers

If you're landing at Suvarnabhumi in the coming days or weeks, expect:

- **Longer taxi queues** than normal, especially during busy arrival periods (typically 6-10 AM and 6-10 PM)
- **Possible refusals** for long-distance destinations — drivers may decline trips to places like Pattaya or Hua Hin if they're concerned about fuel availability on the route
- **Higher demand** for alternatives like Grab, airport limousines, and the Airport Rail Link

## Your Alternatives

Don't panic — getting from Suvarnabhumi to Bangkok is still straightforward:

### Airport Rail Link (Best Option)
- **Price**: 15-45 THB to central Bangkok
- **Time**: 30 minutes to Phaya Thai station (connects to BTS)
- **Runs**: 5:30 AM - midnight
- **Advantage**: completely unaffected by fuel shortages

### Grab / Bolt
- **Price**: 200-400 THB to central Bangkok (varies by distance and surge)
- **Wait time**: may be longer than usual due to driver shortages
- **Tip**: order your ride from inside the terminal before going to the pickup area

### Airport Limousine
- **Price**: 1,000-2,000 THB (fixed rate by zone)
- **Availability**: bookable at counters on the arrivals level
- **Advantage**: guaranteed vehicle, no surge pricing

### Airport Bus
- **Price**: 60 THB
- **Routes**: several lines connecting to BTS/MRT stations
- **Advantage**: cheapest option, runs until late evening

## The Taxi Association's Response

The taxi association is working on proposals to address the crisis, including an **app-based pricing system** that would make operations more efficient. However, implementation requires government approval — and with a newly formed government still settling in after [PM Anutin's election](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-anutin-reelected-thailand-prime-minister/), policy decisions are moving slowly.

## Practical Advice

1. **Don't rely on taxis alone** — have a backup plan (Airport Rail Link or pre-booked Grab)
2. **If taking a taxi**, join the official queue on level 1 — avoid touts offering "special" rides
3. **For Pattaya/Hua Hin/other distant destinations**, consider pre-booking a private transfer or taking the Airport Rail Link to Bangkok first, then booking onward transport
4. **Carry small bills** — taxi meters start at 35 THB and many drivers may not have change for large notes

The fuel situation is expected to gradually improve as [government measures](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-raises-fuel-prices-diesel-capped-33-baht/) take effect, but travelers should plan for reduced taxi availability at Suvarnabhumi for the foreseeable future.
