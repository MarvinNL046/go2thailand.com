Thailand has significantly tightened its visa-free entry policy, reducing the permitted stay from 60 days to 30 days for visitors from over 90 countries, including the United States. The move, according to [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/20/travel/thailand-visa-free-cut-intl-hnk), reflects Thai authorities' frustration with problematic tourist behavior and repeated visa violations.

## Why Thailand Made the Change

Thai immigration officials cited persistent issues with foreign visitors overstaying their visas and engaging in misconduct as the primary reason for the policy shift. The reduction applies to nationals from more than 90 countries that previously enjoyed 60-day visa-free entry privileges. By cutting the allowance in half, Thailand aims to discourage extended stays without proper documentation and reduce the administrative burden of tracking overstay violations.

The decision underscores growing tensions between Thailand's tourism industry and its immigration enforcement. While tourism remains vital to the Thai economy, authorities have grown increasingly concerned about visitors who disregard entry regulations or engage in activities that violate Thai law.

## What Changes for Travelers

The most immediate impact is straightforward: visitors from affected countries now have 30 days to explore Thailand without obtaining a visa in advance, down from the previous 60-day window. This affects millions of annual tourists, including Americans and citizens of other major source markets.

For those planning longer stays, alternatives remain available. Travelers can apply for various visa categories before arrival, including tourist visas, education visas, or retirement visas, depending on their circumstances and eligibility. The [visa section](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/) of go2-thailand.com provides detailed information on available options.

The change may particularly impact backpackers and digital nomads who previously relied on the extended visa-free period to explore Thailand without formal visa applications. Visitors planning stays longer than 30 days will need to plan ahead and secure appropriate documentation before traveling.

## Which Countries Are Affected

While the policy applies to over 90 countries, the specific list includes major tourism sources such as the United States, Canada, Australia, and numerous European nations. Thailand's tourism board has not publicly detailed every affected nation, but the scope suggests that most Western countries and several Asian neighbors are included in the reduction.

Some countries may retain different arrangements based on bilateral agreements or existing special visa arrangements, though these exceptions have not been clearly outlined in available reports.

## What's Next for Thailand's Immigration Policy

This policy change signals Thailand's intent to take a firmer stance on immigration compliance. The reduction may be the first of several measures aimed at improving visitor behavior and reducing overstay incidents. Thai authorities have not announced additional changes, but the move suggests a broader shift toward stricter enforcement.

Travelers planning trips to Thailand should verify current visa requirements well in advance of their departure. Immigration policies can change, and staying informed helps avoid complications at the border. For the most up-to-date information on entry requirements and visa options, consult official Thai embassy websites or Thailand's immigration authority before booking travel.

The change takes effect immediately for new arrivals, so anyone planning a Thai holiday should factor the 30-day limit into their itinerary planning and consider whether they need to apply for an extended visa option based on their intended length of stay.
