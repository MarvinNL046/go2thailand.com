## Background on Songkran Festival

Songkran is Thailand's most vibrant traditional festival, celebrated every mid-April to usher in the Thai lunar New Year. Typically spanning April 13 to 15, it features massive water fights across the country, symbolizing purification and the washing away of the past year's misfortunes. Participants armed with water guns, buckets, and hoses drench each other in streets, turning cities into chaotic battlegrounds of laughter and refreshment amid the hot season.

The festival originated from ancient Buddhist rituals involving water blessings but has evolved into a global spectacle drawing millions of locals and tourists. Major hubs include [Bangkok](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/), where [Khao San Road](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/) becomes a epicenter of foam parties and street splashing; [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/), famous for temple processions and gentle water pouring on elders; and coastal spots like Pattaya and [Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/phuket/), where beachside soaks amplify the fun. Public knowledge holds that Songkran generates significant economic activity through tourism, with hotels, bars, and transport booming.

Alcohol flows freely, music blares, and traffic halts as revelers prioritize play over movement. While widely cherished for fostering community spirit, the event's scale—often described as the world's largest water fight—introduces inherent risks, including slippery roads and crowded conditions.
