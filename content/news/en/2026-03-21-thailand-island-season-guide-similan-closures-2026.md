Planning a Thai island trip? Timing matters — several of Thailand's most spectacular marine destinations **close entirely for months each year** during monsoon season. Here's what's open, what's closing soon, and how to plan around the seasonal calendar.

## Similan Islands: Open Now, Closing May 15

The **Mu Ko Similan National Park** — widely considered Thailand's best diving and snorkeling destination — is open for the 2025-2026 season and will **close on May 15, 2026**. The islands reopen October 15.

During the five-month closure (May 16 - October 14), the nine islands are **completely off-limits to all visitors**. No exceptions, no boats, no permits. According to [Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/life/travel/40056847), the closure serves dual purposes:

1. **Safety**: The southwest monsoon brings rough seas that make boat travel dangerous
2. **Conservation**: Coral reefs, marine life, and island vegetation recover from tourism pressure

**Best time to visit**: Right now through April. Water visibility peaks in **February-April** with temperatures of 26-30°C — ideal for spotting manta rays, whale sharks, and pristine coral formations.

**How to visit**: Day trips and liveaboard diving trips depart from Khao Lak (1.5 hours north of [Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/phuket/)). Book through reputable operators at least a week in advance during peak season.

## Thailand's Seasonal Island Calendar

### Andaman Sea (West Coast) — Season: November to April

These islands follow the Similan schedule, closing during the southwest monsoon:

| Destination | Open | Closed | Best Month |
|------------|------|--------|------------|
| **Similan Islands** | Oct 15 - May 15 | May 16 - Oct 14 | Feb-Apr |
| **Surin Islands** | Nov 1 - May 15 | May 16 - Oct 31 | Feb-Apr |
| **[Koh Lipe](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-lipe/)** | Open year-round* | N/A | Dec-Mar |
| **[Koh Phi Phi](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phi-phi/)** | Open year-round | N/A | Nov-Apr |
| **[Koh Lanta](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-lanta/)** | Open year-round | N/A | Nov-Apr |

*Koh Lipe stays open but some resorts and ferry services reduce operations during monsoon.

### Gulf of Thailand (East Coast) — Season: Year-round (best Dec-Aug)

Gulf islands follow a different weather pattern and generally don't close:

| Destination | Status | Best Month |
|------------|--------|------------|
| **[Koh Samui](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-samui/)** | Open year-round | Feb-Jun |
| **[Koh Phangan](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phangan/)** | Open year-round | Jan-Mar |
| **[Koh Tao](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-tao/)** | Open year-round | Mar-Sep |
| **[Koh Chang](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-chang/)** | Open year-round | Nov-May |

The key advantage of Gulf islands: when Andaman destinations close for monsoon, the Gulf coast often has its best weather.

## Why Seasonal Closures Matter

Thailand's marine park closures aren't bureaucratic inconvenience — they're why the reefs remain worth visiting. As [The Smiling Seahorse](https://www.thesmilingseahorse.com/blog/thailands-marine-national-parks-why-seasonal-closures-are-key-to-their-beauty) explains, parks like the Similans and Surins maintain healthier coral and more diverse marine life specifically because they get months of rest each year.

The contrast with year-round destinations is visible underwater. Reefs at always-open sites like [Maya Bay](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-embassy-land-sales-drive-bangkok-property-prices/) experienced significant degradation before authorities implemented visitor caps and seasonal closures.

## Planning Tips

- **Book Similan trips before April**: availability tightens as the May closure approaches
- **If visiting May-October**: focus on Gulf islands (Samui, Phangan, Tao) for the best conditions
- **Liveaboard diving**: the most immersive Similan experience. 3-4 day trips from Khao Lak cost 15,000-30,000 THB
- **Check before booking**: individual marine parks sometimes adjust dates based on weather conditions. Verify with the [Thai National Parks website](https://www.thainationalparks.com/mu-ko-similan-national-park)

Thailand's island diversity means there's always somewhere good to go, regardless of season. The trick is matching your dates to the right coast.
