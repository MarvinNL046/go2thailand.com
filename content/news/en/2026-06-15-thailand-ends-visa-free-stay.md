Thailand has ended its 60-day visa-free stay programme, according to **www.bangkokpost.com**, signalling a major shift in the kingdom's approach to welcoming international visitors. The decision affects tourists from eligible countries who previously could enter Thailand without obtaining a visa in advance, staying for up to two months without formal immigration documentation.

The termination of this popular visa exemption represents a notable change for the tourism sector, which has long relied on streamlined entry procedures to attract casual visitors and extended-stay travellers. The 60-day visa-free scheme had been a key feature of Thailand's tourism infrastructure, allowing eligible nationals to arrive at airports and land borders and proceed directly through immigration without pre-arranged visas.

## What Changed and Why

The exact date of implementation and official reasons behind the policy change are not detailed in available reports, though such decisions typically reflect broader immigration or security considerations. Thailand's immigration authorities periodically review visa policies to balance tourism promotion with border management objectives.

Travellers who previously relied on visa-free entry will now need to arrange appropriate documentation before arrival. This includes applying for tourist visas through Thai embassies and consulates abroad, or exploring alternative visa categories that may suit their travel plans and length of stay.

## Impact on International Visitors

The removal of the 60-day visa-free option creates new requirements for tourists planning trips to Thailand. Visitors must now determine which visa category best fits their circumstances—whether a standard tourist visa, education visa, or other specialised categories offered by Thai immigration.

Processing times for visa applications vary by location and season, so travellers should plan ahead and apply well before their intended departure dates. Many Thai diplomatic missions offer expedited processing for additional fees, though standard processing typically takes several weeks.

The change may particularly affect:

- **Backpackers and budget travellers** who previously used visa-free entry for extended Southeast Asian tours
- **Digital nomads and remote workers** who took advantage of the 60-day period for longer stays
- **Retirees and long-term visitors** who previously used visa runs or multiple entries
- **Casual tourists** planning month-long holidays

## What's Next for Travellers

Visitors should check the latest requirements on the official Thai embassy or consulate website for their home country. Requirements and processing procedures can vary significantly by location, and immigration policies may continue to evolve.

Travel insurance and comprehensive trip planning become even more important when visa arrangements are required.

Thailand remains a welcoming destination for international tourists, but the administrative landscape has shifted. Prospective visitors should factor visa application timelines and costs into their travel planning, and consult official sources to ensure they have the correct documentation before booking flights or accommodation.
