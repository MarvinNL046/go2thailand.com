Thailand has been ranked the **world's number one destination for solo travelers** in 2026, according to a study by long-haul travel specialists Travelbag that evaluated 36 destinations worldwide on safety, affordability, and friendliness.

## The Rankings

The Travelbag study assessed destinations across multiple criteria relevant to independent travelers. Thailand's combination of low crime rates, excellent transport infrastructure, cultural depth, and budget-friendly pricing earned it the top spot globally.

[Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) scored particularly well, achieving a **daytime safety score of 93 out of 100** — the highest of any city in the study. The northern Thai city's compact old town, established digital nomad community, and walkable neighborhoods make it especially appealing to solo travelers.

## Solo Travel Is Booming

The ranking comes as solo travel experiences unprecedented growth. Research by **Hilton and Virtuoso** found that nearly **76% of Millennials and Generation Z** are planning solo trips in 2026 — a statistic that represents a fundamental shift in how younger generations approach travel.

According to [Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/thailand-tops-the-global-solo-travel-rankings-in-2026-a-safe-diverse-and-adventurous-destination-for-independent-travelers/), Thai Airways has responded to the trend by introducing more flexible booking options that allow passengers to easily modify plans — appealing to solo travelers who value spontaneity.

## Why Thailand Wins

Five factors drive Thailand's top ranking:

**1. Safety infrastructure**
Tourist Police operate 24/7 (hotline 1155), English-speaking assistance is available in major cities, and violent crime against tourists remains rare. Thailand's tourism industry depends on visitor safety and invests accordingly.

**2. Unmatched affordability**
A solo traveler can live well on 1,000–1,500 THB ($28–42) per day in cities like [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) or [Pai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/pai/). Street food meals cost 40–80 THB, and guesthouse rooms start at 300 THB.

**3. Transport connectivity**
Domestic flights, overnight trains, and an extensive bus network make it easy to move between regions without a car. Apps like Grab eliminate the hassle of negotiating taxi fares.

**4. Built-in community**
Thailand's hostels, co-working spaces, cooking classes, and group tours create natural meeting points for solo travelers. You're never truly alone unless you want to be.

**5. Diversity of experience**
Mountains, beaches, temples, nightlife, diving, and jungle trekking — all accessible within a single two-week trip. Few countries offer this range in such a compact geography.
