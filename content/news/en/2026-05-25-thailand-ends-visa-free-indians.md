Thailand has scrapped its 60-day visa-free entry program for Indian citizens, marking a significant change to travel requirements for one of the country's major visitor demographics. Indian travelers can now visit Thailand through the Visa on Arrival (VOA) option, according to **NDTV**.

The policy shift reflects Thailand's broader strategic pivot away from simply maximizing visitor numbers toward attracting what officials describe as "quality tourists." The change comes amid concerns related to security, illegal employment, and transnational crime.

## What Changed for Indian Travelers

Previously, Indian nationals enjoyed a 60-day visa-free stay in Thailand, a benefit that made the country an accessible destination for extended holidays, business trips, and cultural exploration. This generous allowance was one of the more attractive visa policies Thailand offered to Asian visitors.

Under the new arrangement, Indian citizens must now apply for a Visa on Arrival when entering Thailand. The VOA remains a relatively straightforward process for short-term tourism and business visits, though it does require additional documentation and processing compared to visa-free entry.

## Thailand's Strategic Shift

The decision reflects a broader recalibration of Thailand's tourism strategy. Rather than pursuing growth through visitor volume alone, Thai authorities are now emphasizing the quality and profile of incoming tourists. This approach suggests concerns about overcrowding, environmental strain, and social impacts in popular destinations, alongside the security and employment issues cited.

The timing of this policy change indicates Thailand is willing to implement stricter entry requirements to achieve its revised tourism objectives. Such measures can help manage visitor flows to sensitive areas, reduce strain on infrastructure, and ensure that tourism revenue benefits the local economy more directly.

## What This Means for Indian Visitors

Indian travelers planning trips to Thailand should now budget additional time for visa processing and prepare the necessary documentation for a Visa on Arrival application. While the VOA process is generally efficient at Thai airports and border crossings, it does add a procedural step that wasn't previously required.

The change may also affect travel planning timelines. Those accustomed to spontaneous trips or last-minute bookings may need to adjust their approach, though the VOA can typically be obtained relatively quickly for qualifying applicants.

For longer stays or frequent travelers, exploring alternative visa options—such as tourist visas obtained through Thai embassies before departure—may be worth considering, depending on individual circumstances and travel patterns.

## Looking Ahead

This policy modification is part of a wider trend among Southeast Asian nations to refine their visa frameworks. Thailand's emphasis on "quality tourism" suggests the country may continue evaluating and adjusting its entry requirements based on visitor profiles, economic impact, and domestic priorities.

Indian travelers remain welcome in Thailand, and the Visa on Arrival remains an accessible entry pathway. However, those planning visits should factor in the additional administrative requirements and ensure they have proper documentation ready before arrival.

For the most current information on visa requirements, travelers should consult official Thai immigration resources or contact the nearest Thai embassy or consulate before planning their trip.
