Two infrastructure stories that affect travelers in different ways: Bangkok is getting smarter about traffic, while Phuket deals with an environmental cleanup near one of its popular diving destinations.

## Bangkok: AI Traffic Lights Already Working

Bangkok has installed **AI-powered adaptive traffic lights** at **74 intersections** since 2024, and the results are measurable: travel times have dropped by **10% to 41%** depending on location, according to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/nieuws-uit-thailand/thailandblog-nieuwsupdate-algemeen-27-februari-2026/).

The system uses cameras at each intersection to monitor real-time traffic flow and automatically adjust red/green phase timing. Unlike the old fixed-timing system — where lights changed on the same schedule regardless of actual traffic — the AI adapts to conditions on the ground.

The city plans to add **50 more intersections** this year, expanding the smart network across more of [Bangkok's](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/) famously congested road grid.

### What This Means for Travelers

- **Taxi and Grab rides** in equipped areas should be slightly faster and cheaper (less time stuck at lights = lower meter/fare)
- **Sukhumvit, Silom**, and other central corridors are likely priority areas for the system
- **The BTS/MRT** remain the fastest way to cross Bangkok regardless — but for trips that require road transport, conditions are improving

Bangkok's traffic remains challenging by any standard, but incremental improvements like this make a real difference for the millions of daily commuters and the tourists navigating between them.

## Phuket: Oil Spill Cleanup at Coral Island

In less welcome infrastructure news, tar debris from the capsized freighter **Sealloyd Arc** has washed ashore near **Koh Hey (Coral Island)**, located just 8 km from [Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/phuket/). The contaminated zone spans approximately **900 meters** of coastline.

Cleanup efforts are underway:
- Teams are **manually removing tar balls** from the beach
- **Divers are working to seal the wreck** at 60-meter depth to prevent further leakage
- Authorities have warned visitors to **avoid floating containers** that may be contaminated

### Impact on Diving and Snorkeling

Coral Island (Koh Hey) is one of Phuket's most popular day-trip snorkeling destinations, typically visited by hundreds of tourists daily. While the island isn't fully closed, travelers should:

- **Check with tour operators** before booking Coral Island trips — some may reroute to alternative snorkeling sites
- **Avoid the contaminated 900-meter zone** on the island's affected coastline
- **Consider alternatives**: [Phi Phi Islands](https://go2-thailand.com/islands/koh-phi-phi/), Similan Islands, and Koh Racha are unaffected

The cleanup timeline hasn't been specified, but sealing the wreck at 60-meter depth is a complex operation that may take weeks.

## Thai Airways Posts 31 Billion Baht Profit

In aviation news, **Thai Airways** reported a net profit of **31 billion baht** last year and is targeting **5% revenue growth** in 2026. The national carrier is expanding its fleet and increasing coverage on Asian routes.

For travelers, this signals continued investment in Thailand's air connectivity — more routes and competitive pricing on domestic and regional flights. Thai Airways' recovery from its pandemic-era financial restructuring appears firmly on track.
