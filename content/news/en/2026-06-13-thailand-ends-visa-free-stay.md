Thailand has ended its 60-day visa-free stay programme, according to **www.bangkokpost.com**, a decision that will affect how international visitors plan their trips to the Southeast Asian destination. The change represents a shift in Thailand's tourism entry policy and requires travelers to understand the new visa requirements before booking their journeys.

## What Changed

The termination of the 60-day visa-free entry scheme means that visitors who previously could enter Thailand without a visa for up to 60 days will now need to obtain appropriate travel documentation. This programme had been a popular option for tourists seeking extended stays without the administrative burden of applying for a formal visa at a Thai embassy or consulate.

The exact effective date and any transitional provisions for travelers already in the country or with existing plans were not detailed in the available source material. Travelers should verify current entry requirements through official Thai immigration channels before making travel arrangements.

## Background on Thailand's Visa Policies

Thailand has long offered various visa options to accommodate different types of travelers. The 60-day visa-free entry was designed to facilitate tourism and short-term visits, making it easier for international visitors to experience Thai culture, beaches, and attractions without lengthy visa application processes.

The kingdom has periodically adjusted its visa policies based on tourism trends, security considerations, and immigration management priorities. Previous visa schemes have included tourist visas, education visas, retirement visas, and special economic zone visas, each with specific eligibility requirements and validity periods.

## What This Means for Visitors

Travelers planning trips to Thailand will now need to determine which visa category suits their purpose of visit. Options typically include:

- **Tourist Visas**: Traditional visas for leisure travel, usually valid for 60 days
- **Education Visas**: For students enrolled in Thai institutions
- **Retirement Visas**: For foreign nationals aged 50 and above with financial requirements
- **Long-term Visas**: For those with employment, family, or business ties to Thailand

The change may particularly impact backpackers and budget travelers who relied on the visa-free entry for extended Southeast Asian trips. It could also affect digital nomads and remote workers who previously used the 60-day allowance for longer stays.

Visitors should consult the [Thai visa requirements page](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/) for detailed information on current entry options and application procedures. Processing times and documentation requirements vary depending on the visa type and applicant's nationality.

## Planning Your Thailand Trip

With the end of visa-free entry, advance planning becomes more important. Travelers should:

- Determine their intended length of stay
- Identify which visa category matches their purpose
- Allow sufficient time for visa processing before travel dates
- Check with the nearest Thai embassy or consulate for specific requirements

Popular destinations like [Bangkok](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bangkok/), [Phuket](https://go2-thailand.com/city/phuket/), and [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) remain accessible, but entry procedures have become more formal.

The change underscores the importance of checking current travel requirements well in advance of any planned visit to Thailand. Immigration policies can shift, and staying informed ensures smooth entry and an enjoyable stay in the country.
