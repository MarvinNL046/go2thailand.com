Thailand's most ambitious transportation project — a **high-speed rail network** connecting Bangkok to the Laos border — is taking shape, though at a pace that tests patience. Here's where the project stands in March 2026 and what it means for future travelers.

## Phase 1: Bangkok to Nakhon Ratchasima

The first phase of the Thai-China High-Speed Rail project is a **253-kilometer route** from Bangkok to [Nakhon Ratchasima](https://go2-thailand.com/city/nakhon-ratchasima/) (Korat), the gateway to Isaan. As of early 2026:

- **Progress**: 52.46% complete
- **Budget**: 179 billion baht (~$5 billion USD)
- **Delay**: running approximately 28% behind schedule
- **Speed**: designed for trains traveling up to **250 km/h**

The line will have **six stations**: Bang Sue (Bangkok's new central terminal), Don Mueang, [Ayutthaya](https://go2-thailand.com/city/ayutthaya/), Saraburi, Pak Chong, and Nakhon Ratchasima.

When operational, the journey from Bangkok to Korat — currently a 3-4 hour drive or bus ride — will take approximately **1 hour and 15 minutes**.

Two construction contracts have not yet started: the Bang Sue to Don Mueang section (15.2 km) and the Ban Pho to Phra Kaew section (13.3 km), according to [Railway News](https://railwaynews.net/thailands-high-speed-rail-3-airport-project-bangkok-eec.html).

## Phase 2: Nakhon Ratchasima to Nong Khai

The Thai Cabinet approved Phase 2 in February 2025. This **357-kilometer extension** will continue the line from Korat through Isaan to [Nong Khai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/nong-khai/) on the Laos border — where it will eventually connect to the Laos-China Railway, creating a continuous rail link from Bangkok to Kunming, China.

- **Budget**: approximately 341 billion baht (~$9.5 billion)
- **Expected completion**: 2031
- **Stations**: to be determined, likely including [Khon Kaen](https://go2-thailand.com/city/khon-kaen/) and [Udon Thani](https://go2-thailand.com/city/udon-thani/)

## Three-Airport Rail Link

A separate but related project — the **High-Speed Rail Linked 3 Airports** — is creating a **220-kilometer corridor** connecting Bangkok's three international airports:

- **Don Mueang** (DMK) — Bangkok's budget airline hub
- **Suvarnabhumi** (BKK) — Bangkok's main international airport
- **U-Tapao** — the emerging airport serving Thailand's Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC)

This project will be transformative for travelers arriving at different Bangkok airports. Currently, transferring between Don Mueang and Suvarnabhumi requires a taxi or bus ride of 45-90 minutes depending on traffic.

## What This Means for Travelers

**Not yet**: None of these lines are operational. Phase 1 (Bangkok-Korat) is the closest to completion but still years away from carrying passengers.

**When it opens**, the high-speed rail will transform several travel patterns:

- **Bangkok to Ayutthaya**: currently 1.5-2 hours by train → approximately **20 minutes** by HSR
- **Bangkok to Korat** (and Khao Yai): currently 3-4 hours → approximately **75 minutes**
- **Eventually Bangkok to Nong Khai/Laos**: currently 10+ hours by train → approximately **3-4 hours**

**For now**, Thailand's existing rail network remains the way to travel:
- The overnight sleeper train from Bangkok to [Chiang Mai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/chiang-mai/) (13-15 hours) remains a classic experience
- Ordinary trains to [Ayutthaya](https://go2-thailand.com/city/ayutthaya/) from Bangkok's Krung Thep Aphiwat Central Terminal cost just 20 THB
- Domestic flights remain the fastest option for long distances

The high-speed rail project is a long-term bet on Thailand's infrastructure future. For travelers visiting in 2026-2027, conventional trains, buses, and flights remain the practical options — but the infrastructure being built now will eventually make Isaan and the northeast far more accessible to international visitors.
