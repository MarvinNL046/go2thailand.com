If you've noticed that Thailand feels slightly more expensive than it did a few years ago, the Thai baht is part of the reason. The currency has strengthened significantly in 2026, trading in the **31-33 THB per USD** range — compared to 35-36 THB per USD that travelers got accustomed to in 2023-2024.

## Where the Baht Stands

Thailand's Fiscal Policy Office forecasts the baht at approximately **31.8 per USD** for 2026, supported by a weaker US dollar, strong capital inflows, and a healthy current account surplus, according to [Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/economy/40057618).

Several banks expect a **sideways range of 30-32 THB per USD** through the rest of 2026, with [Exchange Rates UK](https://www.exchangerates.org.uk/news/45215/2026-02-12-thai-baht-forecast-2026-best-asian-performer-to-trade-sideways-gold-link-fades.html) noting the baht was the "best Asian performer" in recent quarters.

However, as of late March 2026, the rate sits closer to **32-33 THB per USD** — slightly weaker than the optimistic forecasts, influenced by global uncertainty from [Middle East tensions](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-tourism-7-million-visitors-despite-middle-east-tensions/) and volatile capital flows.

## How This Affects Your Budget

For US dollar holders, the stronger baht means:

| Item | At 35 THB/USD (2023) | At 32 THB/USD (2026) | Difference |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|------------|
| Street meal (50 THB) | $1.43 | $1.56 | +$0.13 |
| Hotel room (1,500 THB) | $42.86 | $46.88 | +$4.02 |
| Daily budget (2,000 THB) | $57.14 | $62.50 | +$5.36 |
| 2-week trip (30,000 THB) | $857 | $937 | +$80 |

Over a two-week trip, the stronger baht adds roughly **$80** to your costs if you're spending in US dollars. For Euro and British pound holders, the impact varies based on their own currency's strength against the dollar.

## Why the Baht Is Strong

Several factors support the baht's strength:

- **Tourism recovery**: [35.5 million visitors in 2024](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-tourism-7-million-visitors-despite-middle-east-tensions/) brought significant foreign currency inflows
- **Foreign investment**: Rising confidence in Thailand's economy is attracting capital into Thai bonds and equities
- **Current account surplus**: Thailand exports more than it imports, supporting baht demand
- **Fed policy**: If the US Federal Reserve cuts rates, the dollar weakens further, strengthening the baht

## Money Tips for 2026

**Best ways to get Thai baht:**
- **ATMs**: Thai ATMs charge a flat **220 THB** per foreign withdrawal. Withdraw larger amounts (10,000-20,000 THB) to minimize the per-transaction impact
- **Wise/Revolut cards**: Offer near-interbank exchange rates with no or low fees — increasingly the best option for travelers
- **Avoid airport exchanges**: Rates are consistently worse than ATMs or fintech cards
- **Credit cards**: Visa and Mastercard offer decent rates but your bank may charge 1-3% foreign transaction fees. Use a no-fee travel card if possible

**Budget adjustments:**
- Thailand remains significantly cheaper than Western countries even with the stronger baht
- [Street food](https://go2-thailand.com/food/) prices (40-80 THB) haven't changed much — baht strength affects tourists more than local pricing
- Consider [shoulder season travel](https://go2-thailand.com/blog/best-time-to-visit-thailand/) (March-May, September-October) for lower accommodation costs that offset the exchange rate impact
- Northern Thailand and Isaan remain the best-value regions regardless of exchange rates

**Monitor rates before your trip:**
- Set up a rate alert on XE.com or your banking app
- If the baht dips to 33-34/USD, that's a good time to convert
- Don't try to time the market perfectly — the difference between 31 and 33 on a typical trip is modest

The bottom line: Thailand is still excellent value for Western travelers. The baht's strength means you're paying roughly 10% more than in 2023, but the country remains dramatically cheaper than home for almost everything — food, accommodation, transport, and experiences.
