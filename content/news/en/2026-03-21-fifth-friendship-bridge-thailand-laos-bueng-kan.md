A new crossing point between Thailand and Laos has opened. The **Fifth Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge** — spanning the Mekong River between [Bueng Kan](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bueng-kan/) province in Thailand and Bolikhamsai province in Laos — began operations on **December 24, 2025**, marking the 75th anniversary of Thai-Lao diplomatic relations.

## The Five Friendship Bridges

Thailand and Laos are now connected by five Mekong River crossings:

| Bridge | Location (Thailand) | Location (Laos) | Opened |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|--------|
| 1st | [Nong Khai](https://go2-thailand.com/city/nong-khai/) | Vientiane | 1994 |
| 2nd | [Mukdahan](https://go2-thailand.com/city/mukdahan/) | Savannakhet | 2006 |
| 3rd | [Nakhon Phanom](https://go2-thailand.com/city/nakhon-phanom/) | Thakhek | 2011 |
| 4th | Chiang Rai | Bokeo | 2013 |
| **5th** | **[Bueng Kan](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bueng-kan/)** | **Bolikhamsai** | **2025** |

## What This Means for Travelers

The new bridge opens up a previously difficult-to-access section of the Thai-Lao border. According to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/nieuws-uit-thailand/nieuwe-vriendschapsbrug-versterkt-band-tussen-thailand-en-laos/), the crossing provides "additional opportunities to discover lesser-known regions" on both sides of the Mekong.

### For Isaan Explorers

[Bueng Kan](https://go2-thailand.com/city/bueng-kan/) is Thailand's newest and most obscure province — established only in 2011. It's home to **Wat Phu Tok**, a temple built on a sandstone mesa with wooden staircases climbing sheer cliff faces, and virtually no international tourism. The new bridge makes Bueng Kan more accessible as a destination rather than just a remote border town.

### For Laos-Bound Travelers

The fifth bridge offers an alternative to the popular Nong Khai-Vientiane crossing (1st bridge), which can be busy. Bolikhamsai province in Laos provides access to central Laos and onward connections to destinations like Vang Vieng and Luang Prabang.

**Important note**: With [Thailand-Cambodia land borders closed](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-cambodia-border-status-travelers-guide/) due to the ongoing border crisis, the Thailand-Laos crossings have become even more important for overland travelers doing Southeast Asia circuits. All five friendship bridges remain fully operational.

### For Visa Runners

The Bueng Kan crossing adds another option for travelers needing to exit and re-enter Thailand. While [DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/digital-nomad-visa/) holders are now limited to 2 visa runs per year, the additional crossing point provides more geographic flexibility for those based in Isaan.

## Beyond the Bridge

The friendship bridges represent more than tourist infrastructure — they're economic corridors connecting Thailand's northeast with Laos and, ultimately, with China via the Laos-China Railway. The vision is a continuous land route from Bangkok to Kunming, with Thailand's [high-speed rail project](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-thailand-high-speed-rail-update-bangkok-nakhon-ratchasima/) eventually linking into this network.

For now, crossing the Mekong on any of the five bridges remains one of Southeast Asia's most atmospheric border experiences — a few minutes over a river, and you're in a different country.
