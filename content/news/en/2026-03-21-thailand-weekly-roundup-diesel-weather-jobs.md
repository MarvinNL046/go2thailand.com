A packed week of developments across Thailand. Here's what travelers, expats, and anyone interested in Thailand should know.

## Diesel Subsidy Ends — Prices Set to Rise

The Thai government confirmed that diesel prices — currently subsidized at **29.94 baht per liter** — will only be held at that rate through **March 16**. After that, prices are expected to rise further (diesel was already climbing from 30 to 33 baht in recent weeks).

The subsidy has been costing the government's Oil Fund approximately **3 billion baht per day**, making it unsustainable long-term. Discussions are underway with refineries about profit margins.

**What this means for travelers:** Rental car and scooter fuel costs will increase slightly. [Grab and Bolt](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-20-grab-bolt-support-drivers-bangkok-oil-prices/) ride fares may also edge higher. Public transport (BTS, MRT, buses) remains unaffected.

## Severe Weather Warning for 29 Provinces

Thailand's Meteorological Department has issued warnings for **29 provinces** across the northern, northeastern, central, and eastern regions. Expected conditions include:

- Thunderstorms and strong winds
- Potential hail in some areas
- Sea waves up to 2 meters (maritime safety alerts for fishers)

**For travelers:** If you're in Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, or northeastern provinces, pack rain gear and check weather apps daily. Island ferry services may be disrupted if waves exceed safety thresholds.

## Thailand Exploring Russian Oil Imports

Deputy Prime Minister confirmed that Thailand is exploring **crude oil imports from Russia**, pending a U.S. sanctions waiver. The government aims to expand strategic reserves from 92 to **98 days** and reduce dependency on Middle East supplies following regional conflict escalation.

Separately, PTT (Thailand's state energy company) is negotiating increased **LNG purchases from the U.S.** — up from 1 million to **1.3 million tons annually** via Cheniere Energy. The first U.S. LNG shipment arrived at Map Ta Phut terminal on January 28 under a contract extending through 2041.

## Job Market Under Pressure

Thailand's Social Security Fund reports that job losses are accelerating. **58,310 workers** lost jobs in January alone, driven by high energy costs and declining exports. K Research projects a minimum of **40,000 job losses** for full-year 2026.

The government is considering support packages for small and medium enterprises to stem the tide.

## Price Controls on 59 Essential Items

The Trade Ministry has established a monitoring list of **59 essential goods and services** due to rising oil prices. The list includes electricity, fuel, meat, food materials, construction supplies, and transport fares. Producers must justify any cost increases before implementing them.

**For travelers:** Basic food and transport prices should remain stable in the near term, as the government is actively preventing inflation from hitting consumers directly.

## Chiang Rai Launches "Fresh Air Operation"

Chiang Rai provincial government has launched a campaign to reduce PM2.5 concentrations, deploying **15 water trucks** and fire department mist systems across **18 districts**. Agricultural burning is banned through May 10.

**For travelers visiting northern Thailand:** Air quality typically worsens from February through April due to agricultural burning. Check [AQI readings](https://aqicn.org/city/chiang-rai/) before planning outdoor activities in Chiang Rai, Chiang Mai, or Mae Hong Son.
