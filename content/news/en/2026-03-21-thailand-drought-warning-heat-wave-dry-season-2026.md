Thailand's annual dry season is shaping up to be particularly severe in 2026, with authorities warning of potential **drinking water and irrigation shortages** from mid-January through May. Combined with temperatures regularly hitting **36-40°C** and the ongoing [burning season](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-chiang-mai-burning-season-air-quality-warning/) in the north, travelers visiting during these months need to plan accordingly.

## The Drought Situation

Thai authorities have instructed all provinces to **stockpile water reserves** and prepare contingency plans for the dry season. The warning is particularly serious for areas outside major irrigation zones — meaning rural provinces, smaller towns, and some island destinations could experience reduced water availability.

According to [ThailandBlog.nl](https://www.thailandblog.nl/nieuws-uit-thailand/thailandblog-nieuwsupdate-algemeen-24-februari-2026/), the concern extends to both drinking water supply and agricultural irrigation, which could affect food prices and availability in some regions.

## Temperature Extremes

Current weather patterns show:

- **Bangkok**: highs of **34-36°C** with 20% chance of thunderstorms
- **Northern and northeastern regions**: temperatures reaching **38-40°C** during peak afternoon hours, with 40% storm probability including potential **hail and heavy downpours**
- **Southern Thailand**: generally more moderate, with sea breezes tempering coastal temperatures

Thailand's hot season (March-May) is always intense, but 2026's drought conditions mean less cloud cover and higher sustained temperatures than normal.

## How This Affects Travelers

### Health Precautions

The combination of extreme heat and [poor air quality in the north](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-chiang-mai-burning-season-air-quality-warning/) creates genuine health risks:

- **Hydrate aggressively** — drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily. Dehydration sneaks up fast in tropical heat, especially if you're active
- **Avoid midday outdoor activity** — plan temple visits, walking tours, and outdoor markets for early morning (before 10 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM)
- **Wear sun protection** — SPF 50+ sunscreen, a hat, and light long sleeves. Thailand's UV index regularly exceeds 11 (extreme) during the hot season
- **Recognize heat exhaustion**: dizziness, nausea, excessive sweating followed by no sweating, confusion. Seek air-conditioned shelter and cool water immediately

### Water Availability

- **Major tourist areas** (Bangkok, Phuket, Koh Samui, Chiang Mai) maintain reliable water supply through municipal systems
- **Smaller islands** and rural destinations may experience reduced water pressure or intermittent supply
- **Drink bottled water only** — never tap water, but this is standard advice year-round in Thailand
- **Some accommodations** in drought-affected areas may request guests to conserve water (shorter showers, reuse towels)

### Storm Awareness

Despite the drought, Thailand's hot season includes sudden **afternoon thunderstorms** that can be intense:

- Storms typically arrive between 2-5 PM and last 30-60 minutes
- **Avoid open areas** during squalls — lightning is a real risk
- The Thai meteorological service specifically warns against sheltering under trees, near billboards, or in open water during storms
- After storms pass, temperatures drop temporarily, creating pleasant evening conditions

## Best Strategy for Hot Season Travel

If you're visiting Thailand between now and May:

1. **Schedule around the heat**: outdoor activities in the morning, air-conditioned museums/malls midday, evening markets and dining after sunset
2. **Choose southern/island destinations** over northern Thailand — the [burning season](https://go2-thailand.com/news/2026-03-21-chiang-mai-burning-season-air-quality-warning/) compounds the heat problem in Chiang Mai and surrounding provinces
3. **Book accommodation with air conditioning** — this isn't the time to save money on fan-only rooms
4. **Take advantage of low-season pricing** — accommodation and flights are [significantly cheaper](https://go2-thailand.com/blog/thailand-cheap-2026-travel-costs/) during the hot months
5. **Carry a portable power bank** — you'll be using your phone more for air quality apps, maps, and ride-hailing to avoid walking in the heat

The drought is a background concern for most tourists rather than an immediate crisis. Thailand's tourism infrastructure is designed for the hot season. But awareness of the conditions helps you make better decisions about where to go, when to be active, and how to stay comfortable.
