Thailand's parliament voted on March 19, 2026 to re-elect **Anutin Charnvirakul** as the country's prime minister, confirming his second term in office after his Bhumjaithai Party won the most seats in the February 8 general election.

## The Vote

Anutin secured **293 votes** in the House of Representatives, comfortably exceeding the 249-vote threshold needed for confirmation. His progressive rival, Natthaphong Ruengpanyawut, received 119 votes, while 86 MPs abstained, according to [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2026/03/thai-parliament-reconfirms-anutin-charnvirakul-as-prime-minister/).

The royal command formally appointing Anutin as prime minister was delivered on March 20 at the [Bhumjaithai Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhumjaithai_Party) headquarters, as reported by [Bangkok Post](https://www.bangkokpost.com/thailand/politics/3220770/royal-command-appoints-anutin-as-thailands-prime-minister).

## Background

Anutin, 58, has served as Thailand's 32nd prime minister since September 2025. His Bhumjaithai Party won **194 seats** in the February 2026 election — the most of any party in the House of Representatives — giving him a strong mandate to form a coalition government.

Before becoming prime minister, Anutin served as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Health, where he gained prominence during Thailand's COVID-19 response. His political approach is generally described as pro-business and pragmatic.

## Policy Priorities

Analysts expect Anutin's second-term government to prioritize:

- **Economic recovery** — addressing household debt and stimulating growth
- **Tourism expansion** — building on Thailand's post-pandemic tourism rebound, which saw [35.5 million international visitors in 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Thailand)
- **Infrastructure development** — continuing major transport and connectivity projects

According to [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-19/thai-leader-anutin-tightens-grip-on-power-after-winning-new-term), Anutin's coalition management and the pace of economic reforms will be closely watched in the coming months.

## Casino Legalization Off the Table

One notable development: the parliamentary vote effectively keeps **casino legalization off the agenda** for now. The entertainment complex bill, which had been debated in previous sessions, did not gain enough coalition support to move forward, according to [AGB](https://agbrief.com/news/thailand/19/03/2026/thailand-vote-secures-pm-anutins-leadership-keeps-casino-legalization-off-the-table/).

## What It Means for Travelers

For tourists and expats, the political transition is largely procedural — Thailand's tourism infrastructure, visa policies, and travel conditions remain stable. The government's continued emphasis on tourism growth suggests that programs like the [DTV visa](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/digital-nomad-visa/) and visa-free entry arrangements will continue.

Thailand's political landscape has seen significant shifts in recent years, but the country's tourism sector has remained resilient throughout. With Songkran 2026 approaching in April, the new government's first major test will be managing the country's largest annual festival season.
