Thailand has ended its 60-day visa-free stay programme, according to **www.bangkokpost.com**, signalling a major shift in the country's tourism entry policies. The decision affects international visitors who previously could enter Thailand without obtaining a visa in advance, a policy that had been a cornerstone of Thailand's tourism accessibility strategy.

## Background on Thailand's Visa-Free Policy

The 60-day visa-free entry scheme had long been one of Thailand's most attractive features for international tourists. The programme allowed eligible visitors from numerous countries to enter Thailand without pre-arranging visa documentation, making spontaneous travel and short-term tourism more convenient. This policy had supported Thailand's position as one of Southeast Asia's most accessible and visitor-friendly destinations.

The termination of this programme represents a notable policy reversal that will require travellers to plan their Thai visits with greater advance preparation. The change affects how millions of potential visitors approach trips to the kingdom and may influence tourism patterns across the region.

## What This Means for Travellers

Visitors to Thailand will now need to obtain appropriate visa documentation before arrival, rather than relying on visa-free entry privileges. This change requires international tourists to apply through Thai embassies or consulates in their home countries or utilise approved visa application services well in advance of their planned travel dates.

The shift impacts various categories of travellers differently. Short-term tourists, business visitors, and casual travellers will all need to adjust their planning timelines. Those who previously booked trips with minimal advance notice will now need to factor in visa processing times, which can vary depending on the applicant's nationality and the specific embassy or consulate handling the application.

For frequent visitors to Thailand, the change necessitates understanding which visa categories best suit their travel patterns—whether tourist visas, multiple-entry visas, or longer-term options. Travellers should consult official Thai immigration resources and their nearest Thai diplomatic mission for current requirements and processing procedures.

## Impact on Thailand's Tourism Sector

This policy change comes at a time when Thailand's tourism industry remains a critical component of the national economy. The decision to end visa-free entry may influence visitor numbers and travel patterns, particularly among budget-conscious tourists and those from countries that previously enjoyed visa-free privileges.

Tourism operators, hotels, and hospitality businesses may need to adjust their marketing strategies and customer service approaches to accommodate the new entry requirements. Travel agencies and tourism boards will likely need to provide enhanced guidance to potential visitors navigating the revised visa landscape.

## What's Next for Visitors

Prospective travellers to Thailand should immediately check the current visa requirements applicable to their nationality through official Thai government immigration websites or their nearest Thai embassy or consulate. Processing times and required documentation vary by country and visa type, so early planning is essential.

Those with existing travel plans should verify their visa status and initiate applications if necessary. The Thai government may provide transition periods or specific implementation dates for the policy change, so staying informed through official channels remains critical.

For detailed information on visa options and requirements, visitors should consult [Thailand's official visa information resources](https://go2-thailand.com/visa/) and contact their local Thai diplomatic mission directly. Understanding these new requirements will help ensure smooth travel experiences and avoid complications at immigration checkpoints.
