Staying connected in Thailand is easier and cheaper than almost anywhere in the world. Whether you choose an **eSIM** (activated before you land) or a **physical SIM card** (bought at the airport), you'll have fast, reliable internet for a fraction of what you'd pay at home.

Here's the complete 2026 breakdown.

## eSIM vs Physical SIM: Which to Choose?

| Factor | eSIM | Physical SIM |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Setup** | Install before departure, active on landing | Buy at airport counter, 10-15 min setup |
| **Price** | From ~$9.90 USD / 10 days | From ~100 THB / 7 days |
| **Phone number** | Some include Thai number | Always includes Thai number |
| **Convenience** | No queue, no passport copy needed | Requires passport, may have queue |
| **Compatibility** | iPhone XS+ and most 2020+ Android phones | Any unlocked phone |
| **Best for** | Prepared travelers, dual-SIM phones | Budget travelers, older phones |

**Our recommendation**: If your phone supports eSIM, go eSIM. You'll be online the moment you land — crucial for showing your [TDAC (Thailand Digital Arrival Card)](https://tdac.immigration.go.th/arrival-card/#/home) QR code at immigration.

## The Three Networks Compared

Thailand has three major mobile operators. Following the DTAC-True merger, the market is effectively two networks, but all three brands continue to sell tourist SIMs.

### AIS (Advanced Info Service)

- **Best for**: Remote areas, island hopping, northern Thailand
- **Coverage**: Strongest signals in rural areas, remote bays, and during boat trips between islands
- **Tourist plans**: AIS Traveller SIM with 15GB for 8 days (~299 THB)
- **eSIM**: Available via AIS app or third-party providers

According to [Thailand eSIM Guide](https://thailandesim.com/mobile-operators/ais/), AIS is the better choice for travelers heading to less-developed areas where signal strength matters.

### DTAC (now part of True Corporation)

- **Best for**: Budget travelers, major tourist centers
- **Coverage**: Excellent in Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, and major tourist areas
- **Tourist plans**: Happy Tourist SIM with unlimited data from ~$9.90 USD / 10 days
- **eSIM**: DTAC Happy Tourist eSIM is widely recommended as the [best overall eSIM for Thailand](https://www.traveltomtom.net/destinations/asia/thailand/best-esim-cards-for-thailand)
- **Bonus**: Often includes free incoming calls and bonus data

### True Move H

- **Best for**: Combined DTAC-True coverage areas
- **Coverage**: Strong in urban areas, merged network with DTAC
- **Tourist plans**: Tourist SIM packages from 299 THB / 8 days
- **eSIM**: Available through True's website and app

## Where to Buy

### Before You Leave Home (eSIM)

1. **Provider websites**: Saily, Airalo, Holafly, SimOptions
2. **Install the QR code** on your phone before departure
3. **Activate on landing** — data starts when you connect to a Thai network

### At the Airport

All major airports have official counters in the arrivals hall:

- **Suvarnabhumi (BKK)**: AIS, DTAC, and True counters on arrivals level
- **Don Mueang (DMK)**: Same three operators available
- **Phuket (HKT)** and **Chiang Mai (CNX)**: Smaller selection but main operators present

Expect to spend **10-15 minutes** for passport registration and activation. Bring your passport — it's required for SIM registration.

### At 7-Eleven

Every 7-Eleven sells prepaid SIM cards and top-up vouchers. Activation takes 5 minutes with passport scanning. This is the most convenient option if you arrive late at night when airport counters may be closed.

## Pro Tips

- **Download offline maps** before arrival as backup — Google Maps allows offline area downloads
- **The TDAC requires internet** at immigration — having data active before landing avoids the scramble for airport WiFi
- **WiFi calling** works on Thai networks if your home carrier supports it
- **Speed**: Expect 20-50 Mbps on 4G, faster on 5G in Bangkok and Chiang Mai
- **Tethering/hotspot**: Most tourist SIMs allow it — useful for laptops at cafés without WiFi
